## 1. The Three Sales Motions at a Glance

| Motion | Buyer | Primary SKU | Sales Cycle | Owner | CAC Target | LTV (3-yr) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Staffing Subscription | IT staffing firms (Talpro India = Customer Zero, plus 200+ mid-tier) | ReadyBank subscription + JD-Forge per-JD | 1–4 weeks | Founder + Bali (self-serve later) | ₹15,000 | ₹3 lakh |
| 2. Enterprise Stack-Vault | GCCs + IT services + BFSI | Stack-Vault annual + JD-Forge subscription | 3–6 months | CEO + AE Enterprise (Month 3 hire) | ₹3 lakh | ₹1.2 crore |
| 3. Platform API | Assessment platforms (HackerRank, Mettl, HackerEarth, iMocha, WeCP, etc.) | ReadyBank API license | 6–12 months | CEO + BD Platforms (Month 3 hire) | $15,000 | $600,000 |

**Why three motions in parallel from Day 1:**
- They have different cash velocities (Motion 1 = Q1 cash; Motion 2 = Q2-Q3; Motion 3 = Q4 onward)
- They serve the same content engine; marginal cost of supporting another motion is near zero
- They de-risk each other (slow Motion 3 won't sink the company; fast Motion 1 funds runway)
- Talpro India = Customer Zero unlocks Motion 1 instantly

**Why NOT to chase any of them out of sequence:**
- Don't build a Platform-only company until Motion 1 + Motion 2 prove the content engine ships at quality.
- Don't build an Enterprise-only company until Motion 1 fills the gross-margin pipeline.
- Don't build a Staffing-only company because the LTV is too low to be venture-grade.

## 2. Motion 1 — Staffing Firm Subscription

### 2.1 ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Tier A (highest-fit, target first):**
- IT staffing firm with 50–500 active candidates per month going through assessment screens
- Already using HackerRank / Mettl / HackerEarth on behalf of end-clients
- Located in India (Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Chennai NCR)
- Decision-maker is Founder / Director / Delivery Head (not gatekept by procurement)
- Annual revenue ₹5–50 crore (mid-tier staffing band)

**Tier B (workable):**
- Smaller staffing firms (10–50 active screens/month) — sell Solo or Team subscription
- Boutique recruiters specializing in tech roles
- L&D consultancies running technical certification

**NOT ICP:**
- Pure non-tech staffing (commercial, blue-collar)
- Very large IT services (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) — they are Motion 2 (Enterprise Stack-Vault) candidates
- Foreign staffing firms with no India operation (Year 2 expansion)

### 2.7 Objections & Responses

**Q: "We already have HackerRank/Mettl. Why do we need this?"**
A: "HackerRank/Mettl are great platforms — we don't replace them. We feed fresh questions into them. Their library is leaked; ours isn't. You keep your existing workflow, just stop using their default questions."

**Q: "Why should we trust an AI-generated question?"**
A: "Fair concern. Every question we ship goes through three human steps: an AI critique, a human SME engineer review, and statistical calibration on a real candidate panel. We don't ship raw AI output — we ship validated content. Want me to walk through the pipeline?"

**Q: "₹14,999/month is a lot."**
A: "Compared to what you spend per Mettl test? Your team probably runs 100+ tests/month at ₹150 each = ₹15K right there, on questions everyone has seen. We cap your cost AND fix the signal quality. The Solo tier is ₹4,999/month if you want to start smaller."

**Q: "What if you go out of business?"**
A: "Talpro Universe is 12 ventures, 7 years, profitable. QOrium is the 13th. Bhaskar isn't going anywhere. Plus your data lives in your CRM and your assessments — you're not locked in to anything."

**Q: "Does it integrate with [Naukri/Talent500/specific ATS]?"**
A: "Today, no native integration — but we export in CSV and JSON which any ATS imports. Year-2 roadmap includes native Naukri + Talent500 integrations. If yours is a critical blocker, let's talk about prioritization."

### 3.7 Objections & Responses

**Q: "₹40 lakh/year is a lot of money."**
A: "It is. Compare to your current spend: 100,000 candidate assessments at ₹50 each = ₹50 lakh/year on a generic platform with leaked content. Stack-Vault is ₹40L flat for unlimited internal use of an exclusive, watermarked, scientifically validated bank. The math works the moment your hiring volume crosses ~5,000 assessments/year."

**Q: "How do we know the questions are actually high quality?"**
A: "We send a 50-question sample pack on your highest-volume role before any commercial conversation. Your engineering panel reviews them. If they don't pass, we don't move forward. That's the gate."

**Q: "What about IP — who owns the questions?"**
A: "You do. Stack-Vault is contractually exclusive — these questions never appear in our shared library, never get sold to another customer, never appear in any JD-Forge output to anyone else. Your contract spells out exclusivity in writing. That's a non-negotiable for us."

**Q: "What if a question leaks externally?"**
A: "Each question in your Stack-Vault is watermarked with a Bosch-specific cryptographic marker. If we detect a leaked question on Glassdoor or LeetCode, we can forensically attribute the source. That's contractually meaningful — both for our remediation SLA and for any internal investigation you'd run."

**Q: "Why not build this in-house? We have a content team."**
A: "How big is your content team, and how many net-new questions did they ship last year? If it's <500/year, we ship more in a quarter for the Stack-Vault tier than your team ships in a year. Plus we bring I/O psychology validation that your team probably doesn't have on staff. The choice isn't 'build vs buy' — it's 'add this layer without growing your team.'"

**Q: "Procurement is going to take 6 months."**
A: "Two paths: One — we start with the JD-Forge Reviewed subscription ($1,999/mo, no procurement gate) for 90 days as a pilot. Bosch evaluates the actual signal quality on real hiring drives. If it works, the Stack-Vault contract is an easier procurement conversation. Two — we work backwards from procurement: send me your standard MSA today, our legal aligns it within 30 days, we're signed by [date]."

**Q: "Why not Mettl, who we already have a relationship with?"**
A: "Mettl is a great platform. We're a content layer that makes their platform better, not a replacement. In fact, your Stack-Vault content can be exported in Mettl import format and live inside your existing Mettl workflow. We complement, we don't replace."

### 3.8 Closing Motion (Stack-Vault Engagement)

The Stack-Vault sales cycle has 5 phases. Average duration 4–5 months.

**Phase 1 — Discovery (Weeks 1-3)**: Bhaskar warm intro → first call → sample pack request
**Phase 2 — Validation (Weeks 4-7)**: Sample pack delivered → engineering panel review → second meeting → scope discussion
**Phase 3 — Scoping (Weeks 8-11)**: Stack discovery (which roles, which depth, which formats) → custom proposal → CHRO + CFO review
**Phase 4 — Procurement (Weeks 12-16)**: MSA + DPA + IP exclusivity contracts → procurement sign-off → PO issued
**Phase 5 — Delivery (Weeks 17-30)**: 8-12 weeks of Stack-Vault build → quarterly refresh begins

The single most important phase is Phase 2 — sample pack acceptance. If the engineering panel doesn't endorse it, no procurement in the world saves the deal.

### 4.3 The Pitch (45-min first meeting)

The Platform pitch is more analytical and longer than the Enterprise pitch. The structure:

1. **Common context (5 min):** Discuss the assessment industry's content economics — $80K-150K per content engineer, 1,800 questions/year per engineer, 15-20% leak rate per quarter, customers demanding constant refresh.

2. **Frame the buyer's specific gap (10 min):** Use research before the meeting — pull from Doc 2 (Competitor Audit) what we know about iMocha's library breadth vs depth. Acknowledge their strengths (English-Pro, conversational, India-stack better than US peers).

3. **The QOrium offer (15 min):**
   - REST API access to a 40K+ question library across 1,000+ skills
   - Anti-leak rotation engine — quarterly auto-refresh of the entire library
   - Multi-format export so iMocha customers can take questions to other platforms (yes, this seems counterintuitive, but it strengthens iMocha's "neutral data hub" positioning)
   - I/O psych validation included — strengthens iMocha's compliance posture
   - $50K Starter / $150K Growth / $500K+ Enterprise tiers; pilot with Growth tier for 6 months

4. **Pilot scope (10 min):** Six-month pilot, $75K commitment, defined success metrics (leak detection saves >₹X content team cost, customer-rating improves by Y, net new skill coverage adds Z). Expansion to full annual contract if metrics hit.

5. **Close (5 min):** Define next steps (security review, sample API call evaluation, technical integration call, contract template).

### 4.7 Objections & Responses

**Q: "We have an in-house content team. We don't outsource."**
A: "Most platforms don't outsource — but the economics of in-house content are getting harder every quarter. Your in-house team can ship maybe 1,800 questions/year per engineer at $100K cost = $55/question. We ship 5,000+ questions/quarter via API for $150K/year, blended cost ~$7/question. We're not asking you to fire your team — we're asking you to give them a force multiplier so they focus on differentiated work."

**Q: "How do we know your content quality is at our standard?"**
A: "We give you a 100-question sample pack across the 5 skills you specify before any commercial discussion. Your content team reviews it like they would review their own backlog. If it doesn't pass their bar, no deal."

**Q: "Our customers expect questions written by us. They'd be unhappy with vendor content."**
A: "Three responses. One — your customers don't know whether your in-house engineers wrote the question or your contracted Indian SME network did; they see 'iMocha question.' Two — every QOrium question can be fully white-labeled and rebranded as iMocha content; we don't insist on attribution. Three — your customers DO care about freshness and signal quality, and that's what we deliver."

**Q: "Anti-leak is interesting but not core to our value prop."**
A: "Today, no — your customers haven't yet articulated it as a buying criterion. Year 2 they will. The first platform to ship 'AI-rotated, leak-monitored library' as a differentiator owns the narrative. Want to be that platform, or react when a competitor lands first?"

**Q: "$150K/year for the Growth tier feels expensive."**
A: "Run the math: $150K / 50,000 questions per month = $0.25 per question delivered. Your in-house cost to author is $50-150 per question. Even after factoring our 6-month pilot price + integration cost, you're saving 200-500x per question on the marginal content."

**Q: "What about IP risk — if we use your questions and one leaks, who's liable?"**
A: "Our standard MSA includes content-warranty clauses: we warrant original authorship + leak monitoring + remediation SLA. Your standard customer contract isn't impacted — if a QOrium question leaks, the rotation engine deprecates and replaces it within our SLA, and you don't carry IP risk."

**Q: "We're already exploring AI content generation in-house."**
A: "Great — building the pipeline yourself is hard. We've been doing this for [N] months and shipped [M] validated questions. ### 5.1 Universal Objection Library

**"How is this different from [generic competitor]?"**
→ Use Doc 2 + Doc 3 to be specific. Do NOT generalize. Name the competitor's specific gap (e.g., "TestGorilla has 350 tests but is shallow on coding depth; HackerRank is great on coding but doesn't cover SAP/Oracle"). Then position QOrium against that specific gap.

**"What's your defensibility against AI commoditization?"**
→ "AI generation is increasingly commodity. What's not commodity: the validated lifecycle around it — anti-leak rotation, I/O psych validation, role-graph organization, per-client watermarking. Anyone can generate a question; nobody else runs the production pipeline."

**"What if Talpro stops investing in QOrium?"**
→ "Talpro India = Customer Zero — Bhaskar can't afford to stop. QOrium is also Talpro Universe's largest single-product venture by ARR potential. Risk is structurally low."

**"Who owns the IP of questions delivered to us?"**
→ Per SKU: ReadyBank licensed (we retain ownership; you have usage rights); JD-Forge IP-protected tier (you own the pack contractually); Stack-Vault fully exclusive (you own the customer-specific library).

### 5.2 Competitive Battlecards

**Vs Mettl/HackerEarth (the giants):**
- "Their platform is great. Their content layer is in-house, slow to refresh, leak-vulnerable. We make their library better, not replace it."

**Vs Glider AI (pure-AI competitor):**

## 6. Pricing & Negotiation Discipline

### 6.1 List Price Discipline

Never discount Stack-Vault Enterprise (₹40L) below ₹35L without CEO approval. The first 3 logos set anchor pricing for the entire market. Discounting early signals weakness.

ReadyBank platform pricing has explicit tier breakpoints; never sell "in-between" tiers. If a customer wants 25K questions/month and Starter is 10K and Growth is 50K, push them to Growth ($150K) — never customize.

JD-Forge per-JD pricing is firm; the only legitimate discount is volume-based subscription (already in the price card).

### 6.2 Negotiation Levers (use in order of preference)

1. **Add value, not discount** — bundle JD-Forge subscription credits into a Stack-Vault contract; bundle ReadyBank starter access into a JD-Forge Enterprise subscription
2. **Multi-year commitment for a small discount** — 2-year Stack-Vault contract = 5% discount; 3-year = 10%
3. **Annual prepay** for 5% discount (improves cash flow)
4. **Reference-customer commitment** for 5-10% renewal discount (must agree to be a public reference)
5. **Last resort: outright discount** — only with CEO approval, only for marquee logos (Bosch, etc.) where the strategic value justifies it

### 6.3 Stack-Vault Pricing Anchor Decision

The CEO has 3 options for Bosch GCC (the first marquee Stack-Vault customer):
- **₹25L (anchor low)** — lower CAC, faster sign, but sets a low industry anchor; harder to charge ₹40L+ to subsequent customers
- **₹40L (anchor mid)** — current default; balanced CAC vs anchor strength
- **₹60L (anchor high)** — slower sign, but if Bosch buys, sets a strong anchor; subsequent enterprises start their negotiation at ₹40L+

CTO recommendation: **start at ₹40L, leave ₹60L on the table for Group-tier discussions.** Document the decision with CEO confirmation before Bosch first meeting.
