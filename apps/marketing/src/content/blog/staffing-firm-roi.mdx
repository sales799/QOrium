## The current cost structure

An IT staffing firm screening 200 candidates per month typically spends in one of two ways:

**Option A — Platform subscription.** HackerRank for Business or Mettl costs ₹15–25K per month. That is ₹1.8–3 lakh per year for the platform alone, plus the time your recruiters spend configuring tests, picking questions from a stale library, and explaining to hiring managers why 90% of candidates passed.

**Option B — Manual screening.** A senior developer spends 30 minutes per candidate on a phone screen. At 200 candidates/month, that is 100 developer-hours — roughly 12.5 working days. At a loaded cost of ₹1,500/hour, that is ₹1.5 lakh per month in developer time. Plus the opportunity cost of what that developer is not building.

Neither option scales. Option A gives you a platform but stale content. Option B gives you signal but burns your best people.

## Where the money actually goes

Break down the assessment spend for a mid-size staffing firm (500 candidates/month):

| Line item                                                                  | Annual cost |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| Platform license (HackerRank/Mettl)                                        | ₹3.0L       |
| Developer phone-screen time (partial — post-platform filter)               | ₹4.5L       |
| Recruiter time configuring and reviewing tests                             | ₹2.0L       |
| Failed hires traced to weak screening (est. 5/year × ₹1L replacement cost) | ₹5.0L       |
| **Total**                                                                  | **₹14.5L**  |

The failed-hire line is the one nobody tracks but everybody pays. When your screen lets through candidates who memorised leaked answers, 1 in 20 makes it to the client, fails the technical round, and costs you a replacement cycle.
