## The proctoring illusion

Assessment platforms invest heavily in proctoring. Eye tracking, screen recording, browser lockdown, copy-paste detection. These tools catch a narrow category of cheating: the candidate who opens a second tab during the test.

They do not catch the candidate who studied the leaked question last night.

That is the category that actually breaks your hiring signal. The prepared cheater looks indistinguishable from a strong candidate. They finish on time. They do not look away from the screen. They do not copy-paste. They simply know the answer already.

## Two layers of the problem

Assessment integrity has two layers:

**Layer 1 — Session integrity.** Is the candidate actually taking the test themselves, right now, without external help? Proctoring handles this. It is a solved problem (with privacy trade-offs, but solved).

**Layer 2 — Content integrity.** Is the question the candidate sees genuinely novel to them? No proctoring tool in the world addresses this. If your question leaked to Reddit three weeks ago and 40% of your candidate pool saw it, your test is compromised before anyone opens the browser.

## How content-level anti-cheat works

QOrium's anti-leak engine operates continuously, not at test time:

**Crawl** — we monitor known leak surfaces (Reddit, GeeksforGeeks, LeetCode Discuss, Glassdoor, Telegram prep channels, GitHub gists) for semantic matches against our library. Not keyword matches — semantic similarity against the question's embedding vector. A paraphrased leak is still a leak.

**Score** — each question carries a leak-risk score from 0 to 100. The score factors in exposure count, recency, platform reach, and whether a solution (not just the question stem) appeared.

**Rotate** — when a question crosses the risk threshold, the engine triggers replacement. The replacement must match the original's IRT difficulty parameter within 0.3 logits and cover the same competency node in the role-graph. The hiring manager sees no change in test quality.

**Watermark** — Stack-Vault questions carry per-customer watermarks. If a watermarked question surfaces externally, we trace it to the source customer within hours.

## The math that matters

A 100-question bank with no rotation has a half-life of about 6 weeks in a high-volume hiring market. After 6 weeks, roughly half the questions have appeared somewhere online. After 12 weeks, the bank is functionally public.

Continuous rotation extends the effective half-life to 6 months or more. The leak surface never accumulates because leaked items are pulled before the next candidate sees them.

## What this means for you

If your assessment vendor talks about anti-cheat and only mentions proctoring, they are solving Layer 1 and ignoring Layer 2. Ask them: what happens when my questions leak? If the answer involves a manual content refresh cycle measured in quarters, your hiring signal degrades every week between refreshes.
