### Decision 8 — Stack-Vault Enterprise Pricing Anchor: ₹40 lakh/year

**Answer: APPROVED — PROCEED.**

**CTO recommendation rationale:** ₹40L/year is the median of the recommended tier range (₹10L Department / ₹40L Enterprise / ₹1Cr+ Group). It anchors above mid-market threshold but below the Group tier ceiling, leaving negotiation room in both directions. Justified by:

1. **Customer math** — Bosch GCC's ~20,000 candidate assessments/year × ~₹500 savings vs current platform spend = ~₹1Cr/year value capture; ₹40L spend = 40% of value, leaving 60% for the customer
2. **CAC math** — ₹3L per engagement CAC + ₹40L revenue = strong unit economics from contract Year 2 onwards
3. **Anchor strategy** — Bosch as Logo #1 sets industry-anchor pricing for subsequent GCC sales; pricing too low (₹25L) compresses anchor for everyone; pricing too high (₹60L) slows Logo #1 conversion
4. **Negotiation floor** — Bali may negotiate down to ₹35L for Bosch as marquee; below ₹35L requires CEO approval per Constitution SO-11

**Action items downstream:** Bali drafts the Stack-Vault Enterprise commercial template with ₹40L anchor + ₹35L negotiated-floor language; CEO signs off on the template; first Bosch proposal uses this template. Owner: Bali + CTO + CEO. ETA: Phase 0 (Day 0–30).
