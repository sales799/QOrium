## 8. NON-COMPETE

### 8.1 During Employment (Strong)

**During your employment with QOrium, you agree not to:**

1. Work for, consult with, or accept equity in a **direct competitor** (defined as any company providing AI-authored assessment questions, JD-specific generation, or stack-based question libraries).
2. Engage in a competing business or freelance activity related to assessment content.

**Scope:** Non-compete applies within India; global if your role has international responsibilities.

**Exceptions:**
- Personal projects or passive investments (approved by CEO) unrelated to assessment
- Prior commitments disclosed at hiring (e.g., advisory board role) remain allowed if they don't interfere with QOrium duties

**Breach during employment:** Grounds for immediate termination for cause (no severance).

### 8.2 Post-Employment (Reasonable, Limited Scope)

**Indian employment law generally renders post-employment non-competes unenforceable** unless they meet the "reasonable restriction" test (per the Indian Contract Act, §27). To maximize enforceability:

**We request, but do not mandate, that you avoid:**

1. Joining a direct competitor for **6 months post-separation** (not 1 year, to be reasonable).
2. Soliciting QOrium customers to the competitor for **12 months** (covered separately in §7).

**This is a non-binding request.** You are legally free to work for a competitor post-separation unless Customer agreement, equity agreements, or non-solicitation clauses (which are enforceable) are breached.

**Recommendation:** Consult your legal advisor regarding your obligations to prior employers or equity holders.

## 11. TERMINATION & SEVERANCE

### 11.1 Termination for Cause

The Company may terminate your employment **immediately, without notice, for cause**, including:

1. **Gross misconduct:** Theft, fraud, dishonesty, violence
2. **Breach of confidentiality:** Unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information
3. **IP theft:** Taking Company code, questions, or other IP without authorization
4. **Repeated policy violations:** Chronic attendance issues, insubordination, regulatory violations
5. **Incompetence:** Failure to meet minimum job requirements after performance improvement plan

**Severance for Cause:** **Zero severance.** Final payment includes earned salary + accrued leave (no bonus or gratuity if termination is for gross misconduct).

### 11.2 Termination without Cause (Redundancy, Restructure)

If the Company terminates your employment **without cause** (business downsizing, role elimination, restructure):

**Notice:** 90 days' written notice (or 3 months' salary in lieu of notice).

**Severance:**
- **Accrued salary:** Paid through notice period (or lump-sum if notice is waived).
- **Accrued leave:** Paid in full (PL, CL, SL encashed at monthly salary rate).
- **Statutory gratuity:** As per Payment of Gratuity Act (if service ≥5 years).
- **Statutory bonus:** If completion of bonus year is pending, pro-rata bonus paid.

**Example:** Terminated in July (mid-bonus year, which ends Dec 31). Pro-rata bonus = (7 months ÷ 12) × full-year bonus.

### 11.3 Resignation

If you choose to **resign**, you are required to provide **90 days' written notice**. During the notice period:

- You remain employed at full salary + benefits.
- Company may request you work during notice or go on "garden leave" (paid, not working).
- You are obligated to transition your work, train successors, and ensure knowledge transfer.

**Severance for Resignation:** Accrued leave + gratuity (if ≥5 years of service). No additional severance or bonus.
