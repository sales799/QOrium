## §2 — Rationale

### Why the original LICENSED path was chosen (v2.0)

When Constitution v1.0/v2.0 was authored (May 1-2, 2026), the assumption was:
- Psychometric content is too capital-intensive to author fresh in Y1
- Licensing from Mettl/SHL/Talogy is the standard path
- Faster to ship; lower regulatory exposure

### Why we're now proposing NATIVELY AUTHORED (v2.1)

After Run #11 strategic analysis (Wave 3 Plan M9+ Kickoff), three considerations changed the calculus:

1. **Differentiation is critical for Stack-Vault Enterprise pricing.** Bosch GCC and similar enterprise prospects are hiring leaders who already use Mettl/SHL/Talogy. If QOrium "licenses" the same content from the same vendors, our Stack-Vault USP becomes "we have a different platform" — which is weaker than "we have different content." Native authoring is the moat.

2. **Vendor lock-in risk.** Licensed psychometric content carries downstream pricing risk: Mettl/SHL/Talogy can raise per-candidate pricing 2-3x annually with limited market alternative. Native authoring eliminates this dependency. Materially impacts Bali pricing discipline (SO-23 anchor).

3. **Authoring economics now feasible.** Wave 3 Plan v0 estimated ₹65L envelope for M3-M9 psychometric authoring (Cognitive Ability + Personality SJT + Aptitude SJT, ~600 Qs). With I/O Psych contractor at ₹1.5L-3L/mo (per C5 SOW) and 6 specialist SMEs at ₹3K-5K per accepted question, the Wave 3 budget is achievable IF Pre-Seed funding closes by M3 OR significant Phase 1 revenue traction.

### Decision Framework score (per Decision-Framework-Reusable-Template-v1)

Scoring the LICENSED vs AUTHORED paths:

| Dim | Weight | LICENSED (v2.0) | AUTHORED (v2.1) |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 Security & Data Protection | 30% | 4 (low risk; vendor-managed) | 4 (we own pipeline; equal) |
| P2 Cost-Effectiveness | 25% | 3 (per-candidate licensing scales linearly; lock-in risk) | 3 (₹65L upfront; amortized over Wave 3 lifecycle) |
| P3 Revenue Impact | 20% | 2 (can't differentiate; commodity) | 5 (true Stack-Vault moat; defensible pricing) |
| P4 Performance & Reliability | 15% | 4 (proven vendor reliability) | 3 (new pipeline; calibration risk) |
| P5 Simplicity & Maintainability | 10% | 4 (vendor handles updates) | 2 (we maintain; ongoing IP risk if poorly authored) |
| **Weighted total** | | **3.30/5.00** | **3.55/5.00** |

**Result:** AUTHORED path scores 3.55/5.00 → APPROVE (threshold ≥3.5).

LICENSED path scores 3.30/5.00 → REJECT (below 3.5 threshold).

## §5 — Risks if amendment NOT ratified

If LICENSED path retained (status quo):
- **Stack-Vault USP weakened** → projected ARR impact $200K-500K/yr by Y2 (estimate; revisit at M3 IdeaForge re-gate)
- **Vendor lock-in compounds** → 2-3x pricing risk on Mettl/SHL/Talogy renewals; Bali pricing discipline (SO-23) harder to defend
- **Differentiation gap** → competitor Stack-Vault-equivalents (rare today; not impossible) could replicate the LICENSED model

If AUTHORED path adopted (this amendment):
- **Capital risk** → ₹65L envelope must materialize via Pre-Seed/revenue by M6
- **Quality risk** → first psychometric items may have lower validity than vendor-licensed; mitigated by I/O Psych contractor + Reference Panel discipline
- **IP risk** → fresh authoring requires IP counsel review per item to avoid derivative-work exposure; mitigated by Constitution Article VII Pillar D + counsel engagement (CC-02)

**Net assessment:** AUTHORED risks are operational + capital-conditional; LICENSED risks are strategic + structural. Decision Framework recommends AUTHORED.
