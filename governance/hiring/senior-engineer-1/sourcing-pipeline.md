week 14. Senior engineering hires in India are slower than SME
content roles — plan accordingly.

## Take-home design (paid)

The take-home is a real piece of QOrium-shaped work, simplified to 4
hours and unique enough that it can't be ChatGPT'd in 5 minutes.

**Format:** ₹10,000 honorarium · 4 hours suggested · 7 days deadline.

**Sample brief (rotate per cycle):**

> You have a pg table `audit_events(id uuid, occurred_at timestamptz,
> tenant_id uuid, actor_id uuid, event_type text, payload jsonb,
> hash_current varchar(64), hash_previous varchar(64))`.
>
> Implement a TypeScript function `verifyAuditChain(events:
> AuditEventRow[])` that:
> 1. Walks the chain in `occurred_at` order
> 2. Recomputes hash_current from canonical JSON of (id, tenant_id,
>    actor_id, event_type, payload, hash_previous) using SHA-256
> 3. Reports per-event status: `intact` / `broken` / `unhashed`
> 4. Handles the legacy case (NULL hash_current on rows from before
>    the hash-chain feature shipped)
>
> Bonus: write 5+ tests covering happy path, single-event tampering,
> missing-link, and mixed legacy + hashed sequence.

This is a real near-clone of `packages/auth/src/audit-hash.ts` (which
shipped in Sprint 4.4.3). It tests:
- TS proficiency
- Hashing primitives + SHA-256 awareness
- Test discipline
- Async / array-walking patterns
- Pragmatic handling of legacy data

A candidate who solves it cleanly with thoughtful tests in 4 hours
is the right shape.

**Alternative — live coding:** offered to candidates who decline
the take-home (some senior ICs decline take-homes on principle). 90-
min live coding via Tuple/Pop or Google Meet screen share.

## Anti-patterns to filter for

- **Ex-FAANG title-rich, code-thin** — long Big-Co tenure but the
  take-home shows shallow code. Decline.
- **CTO-track candidate who wants the title not the work** — wants
  to "lead" but our role is IC for Y1. Decline.
- **Remote-only with no overlap willingness** — we don't need 9-5
  but we need ≥4 hours of overlap with IST for pairing. Hard pass
  on full-async-no-overlap.
- **Doesn't write tests in take-home** — non-negotiable. Decline.
- **Cargo-cults** — uses libraries / patterns without rationale.
  Decline if probing reveals depth gap.

---

## What kills good sources

- Slow response (>7 business days) — senior engineers ghost
- Ghosting in interview — every candidate gets feedback at every
  stage past phone screen
- Disrespect for senior candidate's time — paid take-home is a
  hard line; never run unpaid live-coding > 60 min
- Comp bait-and-switch — our offer matches our band; we never
  "discover budget constraints" mid-process
