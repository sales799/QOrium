# Compensation Benchmark — I/O Psychologist Contractor

**Purpose:** establish a defensible compensation band for the
I/O Psychologist contractor SOW.
**Method:** triangulate Indian academic-applied rates, global
contracting platforms, and analogous senior-IC consulting bands.
**Currency:** INR (primary) and USD (alternate).

---

## TL;DR

| Tier | Profile | Recommended retainer (INR) | Recommended retainer (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Senior** | Ph.D. + 10+ yrs applied; published in IRT/DIF | ₹3.5L–₹4L / month | $5,500–$6,500 / month |
| **Mid** | Ph.D. + 5–10 yrs; OR M.Phil. + 12+ yrs applied | ₹2.5L–₹3L / month | $4,000–$5,000 / month |
| **Junior senior** | Master's + 7+ yrs applied; demonstrated IRT chops | ₹1.8L–₹2.2L / month | $3,000–$3,500 / month |

For 6 months at 10–15 hrs/week, total contract value:

- Senior: **₹21L–₹24L / $33K–$39K**
- Mid: **₹15L–₹18L / $24K–$30K**
- Junior senior: **₹10.8L–₹13.2L / $18K–$21K**

The SOW template (`SOW.md` §5) anchors at **₹18L total / $28K total**
across 6 months — solidly mid-tier. CEO can adjust ±20% on signing.

**Hourly equivalent → monthly retainer:**
- 12.5 hrs/week × 4.3 weeks = ~54 hrs/month
- At ₹6K/hr (mid) → ₹3.24L/month
- At ₹4K/hr (junior senior) → ₹2.16L/month
- At ₹8K/hr (senior) → ₹4.32L/month

The retainer band above falls inside this implied hourly range, with
a slight discount for committed retainer vs. ad-hoc project work
(retainer trades hourly upside for predictability — typical 10-15%
haircut applies).

## Comparator: what the market pays for nothing-equivalent

To stress-test the band, here are 3 not-quite-equivalent comparators:

| Comparator | Approx rate | Why it's not equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Pearson VUE psychometric consultants (per-project) | $200-$300/hr | Pearson VUE has scale + history; we're a startup; we cannot pay Pearson rates |
| University ad-hoc consulting (TISS, IIT-B) | ₹3K–₹6K/hr | Lower scope, no anti-leak/confidentiality regime |
| Indian boutique HR-tech (Wheebox, CoCubes) consulting fees | ₹4K–₹7K/hr | Closer to our use-case; our band aligns |

**Conclusion:** the recommended band is defensible — neither
underpaying (which would limit candidate quality) nor overpaying for
a Year-1 startup (which would burn cash on the human lane).

## Negotiation guidance

When a candidate gives a rate counter:

| Counter | Response |
|---|---|
| Within ±10% of band | Accept; book the SOW |
| +10% to +25% | Offer +10% in exchange for a longer term commitment (12 months instead of 6) OR an additional deliverable (e.g., contributing to the investor data-room methodology section) |
| +25% to +50% | Decline politely; "we're set on a band, but we'd love to revisit at month-6 renewal at this rate" |
| > +50% | Decline; not the right contractor for this stage |
| Below band | Don't underpay — raise to mid-band even if they accept lower; this preserves quality + signals respect |

**Signing bonus:** the SOW allows a 10% signing bonus, deductible from
the final milestone. Use this to bridge a 5-10% gap if the candidate
is otherwise the right fit and is cash-flow sensitive.

## Currency + tax notes

- **India-resident contractor:** pay in INR via NEFT/RTGS to a registered
  proprietorship/LLP. Apply 10% TDS under section 194J (professional
  services) unless contractor presents a lower-rate certificate. Issue
  Form 16A on quarterly basis.
- **US-resident contractor:** pay in USD via wire or Wise. No US
  withholding for non-US-source services performed outside the US;
  contractor handles their own self-employment tax. Issue Form 1099-NEC
  at year-end if total > $600.
- **UK / EU / Singapore-resident contractor:** consult counsel; PE
  (permanent establishment) risk needs review if contractor visits
  India for the engagement.

GST: Indian contractor charges 18% GST on professional services; QOrium
pays + claims input credit. Non-Indian contractor's services qualify
as "import of services" — QOrium pays IGST under reverse charge and
claims input credit.
