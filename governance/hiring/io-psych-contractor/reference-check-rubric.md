# Reference-Check Rubric — I/O Psychologist Contractor

**Format:** 30-minute structured phone reference call.
**Who calls:** CEO (primary) + CTO Office (silent observer/notetaker).
**How many references:** Minimum 2; ideally 3 (1 academic + 1 applied + 1 prior client).
**When to use:** Before signing the SOW, after first-call + second-call
have produced a preferred candidate.

The rubric below is **for note-taking, not for reading aloud**. Open
the call with the reference's preferred conversational pacing.

---

## Pre-call setup (5 min)

| # | Step |
|---|---|
| 1 | Ask candidate for 3 references with email + relationship |
| 2 | Send a 2-line note to each reference: "I'm checking on Dr X for a 6-month consulting engagement; 30-min call OK?" |
| 3 | Schedule via Calendly/email; pre-share QOrium 1-pager + role summary |
| 4 | Reference signs a verbal NDA at start of call ("Everything you share stays with me + CTO; we won't quote you to the candidate") |

---

## Question Set (12 questions, 90-120 sec each)

### Background (2 questions)

**Q1.** "How do you know Dr [Candidate]? In what context did you work
together?"
> *Listening for:* concrete project, duration, intensity. Vague
> descriptions ("we crossed paths at a conference") are a soft yellow
> flag.

**Q2.** "Over what period of time?"
> *Listening for:* recency. References from > 5 years ago are dated;
> ideally last 2-3 years.

### Methodological depth (3 questions)

**Q3.** "Have you seen them work hands-on with item-response theory or
modern psychometric calibration?"
> *Listening for:* specific examples — software used (R/lavaan, Mplus,
> Stata, custom Python), datasets handled, problems solved. Vague
> "they're up to date on modern psychometrics" is yellow.

**Q4.** "Tell me about a time their methodological judgment was
challenged. How did they handle it?"
> *Listening for:* willingness to revise, evidence-based pushback, NOT
> dogmatism. Red flag: "they always insisted they were right."

**Q5.** "If you had to describe their approach to bias detection in
employment testing in two sentences, what would you say?"
> *Listening for:* nuance — DIF is necessary but not sufficient; need
> qualitative review too; awareness of intersectional bias. Red flag:
> "they think DIF is over-stated" or "they don't really do bias work."

### Reliability + delivery (3 questions)

**Q6.** "How were they on deadlines and deliverables?"
> *Listening for:* specific examples. Generic praise ("very reliable")
> is fine but probe with Q7.

**Q7.** "Was there ever a deliverable that slipped or that you had to
chase? What happened?"
> *Listening for:* honest answer. Everyone slips occasionally; the red
> flag is "no, never" (likely sanitising) OR "frequently, and they
> didn't communicate" (real red flag).

**Q8.** "How do they handle scope changes or changing requirements
mid-engagement?"
> *Listening for:* flexibility within reason; not "every change is a
> change-order conversation"; not "they accept anything and overload
> themselves."

### Independent practice (2 questions)

**Q9.** "Are they comfortable working asynchronously, with primary
contact via email/Slack, and minimal real-time meetings?"
> *Listening for:* yes, with concrete examples. QOrium's engagement
> is async-first; not a fit for someone who needs daily face time.

**Q10.** "What's their style when they disagree with the client's
direction?"
> *Listening for:* direct + respectful + evidence-based. Red flag:
> passive-aggressive ("they'll do it but you'll know they hate it")
> or absent ("they just go along").

### Confidentiality + professional ethics (2 questions)

**Q11.** "Have you ever had any concerns about how they handled
sensitive or confidential information?"
> *Listening for:* clean answer. Any hesitation here is a stop signal
> for QOrium given Wave-1/2/3 anti-leak discipline.

**Q12.** "Final question — would you hire them again, or refer them
to a friend who needed similar work? Why or why not?"
> *Listening for:* unhesitating yes is the strongest signal. "I would,
> with one caveat..." is fine if the caveat is small. "I'm not sure"
> or hesitation is a serious flag.

## Scoring guide

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Outstanding — concrete + specific, multiple positive examples |
| 4 | Strong — solid endorsement, no concerns |
| 3 | Mixed — endorsed but with non-trivial caveats |
| 2 | Concerning — vague or guarded answers, soft red flags |
| 1 | Negative — clear red flags, do not proceed |

Score each of Q3–Q12 (10 questions). Q1 + Q2 are setup, not scored.

**Decision matrix:**

| Total | Action |
|---|---|
| 45–50 | Strong proceed; SOW within 5 business days |
| 38–44 | Proceed with second reference call; close on SOW pending no contradictions |
| 30–37 | Proceed only if other references compensate; document concerns in onboarding doc |
| ≤ 29 | Decline with thanks; no SOW |

If Q11 (confidentiality) scores ≤ 3, decline regardless of total —
QOrium's anti-leak discipline is non-negotiable.

---

## Post-call write-up

Within 24 hours, draft a 1-page note covering:

1. Reference name + relationship + length of association
2. Top 3 strengths heard
3. Top 1-2 concerns heard
4. Score per question (Q3–Q12) + total
5. Recommendation: PROCEED · PROCEED-WITH-CAVEAT · DECLINE

Save to `governance/hiring/io-psych-contractor/reference-checks/`
(folder created on first reference call). One file per reference.

CEO + CTO Office review before final SOW signing decision.

---

## What references should NOT be asked

- Salary at prior engagements (privacy)
- Personal life, family, health
- Compensation expectations (ask the candidate directly)
- Anything about protected categories (caste, religion, gender,
  marital status, etc. — illegal under Indian + most jurisdictions'
  hiring law and ethically out-of-bounds)

---

_Reference checks are gated on candidate consent. Candidate must
provide references in writing; QOrium contacts only the references
named by the candidate._
