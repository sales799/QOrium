The role in one paragraph

You own the quality gate between authored and released. Every question that touches a candidate carries your signoff. You build the review process, hire and lead the Y2 review team, and own the roadmap for Wave 1 (Tech Core), Wave 2 (India Stack), Wave 3 (Psychometric), and Wave 4 (Domain Specialist).

First 90 days

  • Audit the 986 already-authored items against Bloom's taxonomy + construct validity + face validity + bias
  • Promote ~85–90% of those to status='released'; revise or retire the rest
  • Co-author the Wave-3 (Psychometric) authoring template alongside the I/O Psychologist contractor
  • Define the quality rubric that all future authors work against
  • Set up the SME review queue cadence (daily for first 90 days, weekly thereafter)

Months 4–12

  • Lead the Wave-3 ramp to 200 items
  • Hire 2–3 sub-skill specialists (Y2; QOrium funds the seats)
  • Define and ship the Wave-4 specification (Domain Specialist — banking, healthcare, automotive, semiconductor — your call on which 2–3 to start)
  • Co-author QOrium's public methodology white paper (with the I/O Psych contractor)
  • Sit on the customer Trust page; your photo + 1-line credential block appears with attribution
  • Participate in 2–3 customer prospect calls per quarter as a credentialed voice

Required

  • 8+ years in technical assessment authoring, exam item development, or equivalent (AICTE/NSDC certifications, Pearson VUE, ETS, AspiringMinds/SHL, NIIT certification programs, IIT/IIM entrance prep authoring)
  • Strong methodological grounding: Bloom's taxonomy, IRT basics (you don't have to derive 3PL but you should know what discrimination + difficulty + guessing parameters mean)
  • Demonstrated experience reviewing items for face validity, bias, and ambiguity at scale (≥500 items in your career)
  • Ability to articulate why an item works or doesn't in 2–3 sentences
  • Comfort with async-first, written-decision-trail working style
  • Ownership mindset

Process

  1. Application review (we read every CV)
  2. Round 1 (45 min) — methodology conversation with CTO Office
  3. Round 2 (90 min) — portfolio review + live item-review exercise
  4. Round 3 (60 min) — CEO conversation + culture fit

End-to-end ≤ 4 hours of your time.
