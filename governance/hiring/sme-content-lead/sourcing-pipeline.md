on 1 by week 10. **Channel mix:** 5 channels in parallel, weighted by
expected yield × cost-per-qualified-candidate.

## JD-Forge alpha screening (dogfooding)

For applicants from channels 2 (LinkedIn/Naukri), we use **JD-Forge
alpha** as a first-round screening tool. This is dogfooding our own
product and serves three goals:

1. We test our own product on a real flow before customer demos
2. The screener is itself a calibrated IRT-style mini-assessment
3. We collect signal on JD-Forge's UX from senior candidates

**The screener:** 12 items, 30-minute timed, mix of MCQ + short
written. Topics: Bloom's taxonomy application, item construction
review, bias detection scenarios, IRT parameter interpretation.

**The pass mark:** 80% (calibrated against our internal team's
performance — CTO Office baseline = 92%, expected SME Lead candidate
= 85-95%).

**Friction risk:** senior candidates may bristle at a take-home
screener. Mitigation:
- Frame as "5-minute calibration check" not "test"
- Share that the result feeds our internal calibration too (not just
  one-way)
- Pay ₹5,000 for completion regardless of pass/fail (per JD process
  discipline section)
- Skip the screener for candidates from channels 1, 3, 4, 5 (direct
  outreach + recruiter + referral + community) — those are
  trust-bypass channels

**Implementation:** uses the existing `services/readybank` admin
console; the screener is a closed test session with results visible
only to CTO Office and CEO. JD-Forge integration is a follow-on
sprint; for now, a manually-administered version works.

## Dropbox: what kills good sources

- Slow response: applicants ghost if we don't respond in 7 business
  days. JD process discipline section commits us to that.
- Ghosting in interview: every applicant gets feedback at every
  stage past the first phone screen. Word travels.
- Disrespect for senior candidates' time: no take-home > 4 hours
  unpaid; our ₹5K/4h pay is non-negotiable.
- Poorly-briefed recruiter: if the recruiter sends 3 weak candidates
  in week 2, replace them in week 3.
