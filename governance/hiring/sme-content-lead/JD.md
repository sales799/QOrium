## About QOrium

QOrium builds **ReadyBank** — an IRT-calibrated, anti-leak-rotated,
watermark-per-candidate question library for technical hiring. We
ship 5,000 high-stakes assessment items across 13 sub-skills (Java,
Python, React, SQL/Data, DevOps/SRE, Salesforce, AWS, AIPE, SAP-ABAP,
Oracle HCM, CPQ, Finacle, Embedded Automotive). Today we have 1,300
authored and 1,300 calibrated; our target by Y1 close is 5,000.

We sell to recruiters (Talpro is Customer-Zero), staffing platforms
(Stack-Vault SKU), and direct enterprise hiring teams (JD-Forge SKU).

We are a third-time-founder team backed by a Year-1 budget, with a
public commitment to **never ship a question we can't defend
methodologically**. That's where you come in.

## The role in one paragraph

You own the quality gate between **authored** and **released**. Every
question that touches a candidate carries your signoff. You build the
review process, hire and lead a team of reviewers (Y2), and own the
roadmap for Wave-1 (Tech Core), Wave-2 (India Stack), Wave-3
(Psychometric), and the future Wave-4 (Domain Specialist).

## What you'll do (first 90 days)

- Audit the 1,300 already-authored items against Bloom's taxonomy +
  construct validity + face validity + bias considerations
- Promote ~85-90% of those to `status='released'`; revise or retire
  the rest
- Co-author the Wave-3 (Psychometric) authoring template alongside
  the I/O Psychologist contractor
- Define the quality rubric that all future authors work against
- Set up the SME review queue cadence (daily for first 90 days,
  weekly thereafter)

## What you'll do (months 4-12)

- Lead the Wave-3 ramp to 200 items
- Hire 2-3 sub-skill specialists (Y2; QOrium funds the seats)
- Define and ship the Wave-4 specification (Domain Specialist:
  banking, healthcare, automotive, semiconductor — your call on
  which 2-3 to start)
- Co-author QOrium's public methodology white paper (with the I/O
  Psych contractor)
- Sit on the customer Trust page; your photo + 1-line credential
  block appears with attribution
- Participate in 2-3 customer prospect calls per quarter as a
  credentialed voice

## What we're looking for

### Required

- 8+ years in technical assessment authoring, exam item development,
  or equivalent (e.g., AICTE/NSDC certifications, Pearson VUE, ETS,
  AspiringMinds/SHL, NIIT cert programs, IIT/IIM entrance prep
  authoring)
- Strong methodological grounding: Bloom's taxonomy, IRT basics
  (you don't have to derive 3PL but you should know what
  discrimination + difficulty + guessing parameters mean)
- Demonstrated experience reviewing items for face validity, bias,
  and ambiguity at scale (≥500 items in your career)
- Ability to articulate why an item works or doesn't in 2-3 sentences
- Comfort with async-first, written-decision-trail working style
- Ownership mindset: you are the quality gate; you don't pass the
  buck

### Strong to have

- Domain knowledge in 2+ of QOrium's Wave-1 sub-skills (Java, Python,
  React, SQL, DevOps, Salesforce, AWS, AIPE)
- Direct experience authoring or reviewing for HackerRank, Codility,
  Mettl, AspiringMinds, NSDC, AICTE, or similar
- Experience hiring + leading a small content team
- Comfort writing for a public audience (white paper section
  authorship)

### Nice to have

- Postgraduate degree in education, psychometrics, computer science,
  or relevant domain
- Open-source contributions or content in your portfolio
- Conference talks, papers, or recognized authorship

### What we're NOT looking for

- Pure HR generalists with no item-development credentials
- LMS administrators (you should have authored, not configured)
- Sales-engineering-style "content marketers" — this is a deep
  authorship + review role, not GTM

## How you'll be evaluated

The interview process tests four things:

1. **Methodological depth** — can you explain why item X is poorly
   constructed and how you'd fix it?
2. **Volume judgement** — can you make 50 release/revise/retire
   decisions per day without quality drift?
3. **Author leadership** — can you give an author actionable
   feedback that doesn't crush morale?
4. **Communication** — can you defend a decision in writing to a
   sceptical customer?

See `interview-process.md` for the stage gates.
