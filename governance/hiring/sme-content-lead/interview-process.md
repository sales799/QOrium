# Interview Process — SME Content Lead

5 stage gates · ~6-8 hours of candidate time · ~5-6 hours of QOrium
time per candidate. Aim for 6-8 weeks from application to offer.

---

## Stage 0 — Application screen (CEO + CTO, 15 min/applicant)

**Goal:** filter to phone-screen candidates.

Inputs:
- CV
- 1-page note ("tell us about an item you authored or rejected")
- LinkedIn URL

Filter for:
- ≥ 8 years in technical assessment authoring or item development
- Specific artifact mention (an item, a methodology, a course)
- Coherent writing in the 1-page note

Reject (politely, within 7 business days):
- < 5 years experience
- Pure HR generalist with no item-development credential
- 1-pager that's a CV-restate (not a thoughtful note)

**Pass-through rate target:** 25-35%.

---

## Stage 1 — Phone screen (CTO Office, 30 min)

**Goal:** confirm fit + interest; screen for red flags.

Format: 30-min Google Meet.

Questions (sample):

| # | Question | What we listen for |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Walk me through one item you wrote that you're proud of. Why? | specificity; methodological awareness |
| 2 | Walk me through one item you rejected. Why? | judgment + ability to articulate |
| 3 | What kind of feedback have you given authors that they didn't like at first? | leadership instinct |
| 4 | What's your current understanding of IRT? | depth indicator |
| 5 | Why this role (vs. staying where you are)? | motivation alignment |
| 6 | What's your timeline for making a decision? | urgency calibration |

End-of-call:
- If yes: schedule Stage 2 (take-home D1 + D2)
- If no: written feedback within 3 business days

**Pass-through rate target:** 40-50%.

---

## Stage 2 — Take-home (D1 + D2 from `screening-rubric.md`)

**Goal:** test methodological depth + volume judgement.

What candidate receives:
- 4-item review exercise (D1)
- 30-item batch review exercise (D2)
- Anonymized version of QOrium Wave-1 / Wave-2 sample items (no
  question IDs; cannot be cross-referenced to live library)
- ₹5,000 honorarium for completion regardless of outcome

Time: 5 days to submit; 1.5-2 hours actual work.

Scoring: per `screening-rubric.md` D1 + D2.

Pass criterion: D1 ≥ 4 AND D2 ≥ 4.

**Pass-through rate target:** 50-60% of who reach this stage (less
than 30% from initial application).

---

## Stage 3 — Deep panel (CEO + CTO Office, 90 min)

**Goal:** test leadership + communication + cultural fit.

Format: 90-min Google Meet OR in-person if Mumbai-based.

Structure:

| Time | Module | Run by |
|---|---|---|
| 0-10 min | Welcome + candidate Q&A | CEO |
| 10-40 min | D3 author leadership role-play (live) | CTO Office |
| 40-70 min | D4 customer email essay (live; 30-min uninterrupted writing) | CTO Office observes; candidate writes |
| 70-90 min | Wrap-up: candidate Qs + next-step disclosure | CEO |

Scoring: per `screening-rubric.md` D3 + D4.

Pass criterion: D3 ≥ 4 AND D4 ≥ 4 AND total D1-D4 ≥ 16.

**Pass-through rate target:** 60-70% of who reach this stage.

---

## Stage 4 — Reference checks (CEO, 30 min × 2-3 references)

**Goal:** confirm what we've seen; surface anything we missed.

Inputs: 2-3 references (candidate provides; consent confirmed in
writing).

Use the rubric pattern from
`governance/hiring/io-psych-contractor/reference-check-rubric.md` (12
questions, 5-dimension scoring) — adapted for SME Lead role.

Pass criterion: no concerning answer on confidentiality, ethics, or
delivery; no "would not hire again" answer.

**Pass-through rate target:** 90%+ of who reach this stage. If a
reference surfaces concerning info, escalate to CEO + CTO for a
hold-or-proceed decision.

---

## Stage 5 — Final + offer (CEO + CTO, 30 min)

**Goal:** decide + extend offer + close.

Format: 30-min decision call (CEO + CTO Office; candidate not
present); then 30-min candidate call (CEO + CTO Office + candidate)
to extend offer + answer any final questions.

Decision criteria:
- All Stage 2-4 criteria met
- No outstanding concerns
- Compensation conversation has happened (target offer is set)

Offer:
- Written letter via email
- Salary band + ESOP grant + benefits per `compensation-band.md`
- 7-day acceptance window
- Pre-acceptance: candidate gets a virtual coffee with one current
  team member (CTO Office, on the basis "ask anything")

If candidate accepts:
- Background check initiated (standard)
- Notice period typically 1-3 months in India; we plan accordingly
- Day-1 onboarding doc prepped during notice period

If candidate declines:
- 30-min retro call within 7 days: what would have changed their
  mind? Use to refine sourcing + comp + role for future hires
- Add to "alumni network" — re-engage in 12 months if still
  unfilled or open scope changes

## Calendar discipline

| Stage | Target SLA from prior stage |
|---|---|
| 0 → 1 | 5 business days |
| 1 → 2 | 3 business days |
| 2 → 3 | 7 business days (candidate has 5 to submit; 2 to score) |
| 3 → 4 | 5 business days |
| 4 → 5 | 3 business days |
| **Total target** | **23 business days = ~5 calendar weeks** |

Slack the candidate proactively if any stage exceeds SLA. Senior IC
candidates have other offers; speed wins.

---

## Hiring panel

| Role | Stage involvement | Decision weight |
|---|---|---|
| CEO | 0, 1, 3, 4, 5 | tie-breaker; final yes |
| CTO Office | 0, 1, 2, 3, 5 | technical / methodology lead |
| I/O Psych contractor (when signed) | 3 (consultative only) | adviser; no veto |
| Talpro Delivery Head | 5 (consultative if available) | adviser |

CEO and CTO Office must both say yes to extend an offer. A single
veto from either kills the candidate.

---

## Rejection discipline

Candidates who reach Stage 1 (phone screen) or beyond receive
written rejection feedback within 3 business days, structured as:

- 1 paragraph: what we appreciated
- 1 paragraph: what tipped the decision
- 1 paragraph: door-open language ("if [thing] changes, please
  reach back")

Stage 0 rejects get a 2-line acknowledgement only.

Word travels in the Indian assessment community. Rejecting badly is
the fastest way to poison future hiring.

---

## Equity / fairness controls

- Same questions, same rubric, same time budgets for every candidate
- Score independently before debriefing (CEO + CTO score privately,
  then compare)
- No "culture fit" override of rubric scores; if CEO + CTO want to
  hire someone whose rubric is < 16, the decision is logged with
  written rationale
- Counter-rotating note-taking across candidates so write-ups are
  consistent
- Annual review: have we hired diversely? Where are we losing
  candidates? Adjust process accordingly
