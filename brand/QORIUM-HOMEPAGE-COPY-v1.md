## Section 2 — The leak story (villain)
- **Eyebrow:** Why most question banks fail
- **H2:** Your question bank is rotting. You just can't see it yet.
- **Body:** A fresh question gets screenshotted, indexed by prep sites, and taught as a pattern — inside the same hiring year you bought it. By month nine, pass rates spike, the signal's gone, and you're trusting a score that's already been gamed.
- **Timeline (keep the three steps; copy below):**
  - **Week 1–4 — First exposure.** Early candidates sit the question. A few post screenshots or memory-reconstructed versions.
  - **Month 2–6 — The prep market catches up.** Aggregators and coaching services start teaching the exact answer.
  - **Month 6–9 — Signal collapse.** Pass rates spike, the item is burned, and the result stops meaning anything.
- **Closing line:** This is the problem QOrium is built to beat — not hide.

## Section 4 — The moat as buyer outcomes (taxonomy → benefit)
- **Eyebrow:** What keeps your tests honest
- **H2:** Eight things that keep your scores trustworthy. Most vendors do one or two.
- **Items (benefit-led; internal name as small tag only):**
  1. **Fresh items on demand** *(AI-authored pipeline)* — New questions generated and validated, so you're never stuck reusing a burned one.
  2. **Validated, not just written** *(I/O-psych validation)* — Items run through a psychometric check before they reach a candidate.
  3. **Questions that retire before they leak** *(anti-leak rotation)* — We scan, retire, and regenerate on a cycle, so the bank can't go stale on you.
  4. **Use the score anywhere** *(multi-format export)* — Pull results into your ATS or workflow without rebuilding anything.
  5. **Tests for the stack you actually run** *(India-stack depth)* — Real depth in SAP, Oracle, ABAP, and the enterprise stacks others skip.
  6. **Organised by the role you're hiring** *(role-graph)* — Questions mapped to role × skill × difficulty, so the pack fits the job.
  7. **Every leak traces back** *(per-candidate watermarking)* — Each candidate sees a uniquely watermarked variant; if it surfaces online, you know the source.
  8. **Built to plug in** *(content API first)* — License the content through an API instead of staffing an authoring team.
