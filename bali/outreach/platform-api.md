## Objection handling (cross-reference Bali Playbook §8)

**O3 — "Why can't we just prompt GPT-5 ourselves?"**
_Answer:_ Validation is the moat. Our 7-stage pipeline includes IRT calibration on a Reference Panel and SME validation by I/O psychologists. Raw GPT output fails our auto-fail criteria (per Constitution Article VII). Talpro India ran that experiment in M0 — we ship the output. Want a 15-min call with their TA lead?

**O4 — "Mettl/HackerRank already work fine for us."**
_Answer:_ You're a platform. They're a platform. The question is who supplies the next 100K items at calibrated quality with daily anti-leak. Not them. Us. Our pricing band is intentionally below build-it-yourself + half of partner-with-them.

**O6 — "We need SOC 2 Type II before we sign."**
_Answer:_ SOC 2 is in progress per our roadmap on `qorium.online/security`. We can sign a DPA today, scope the pilot under contractual security commitments, and complete SOC 2 attestation by close. Talpro India operates under DPDPA-ready posture in production.

## Follow-up cadence

- **Day 0:** Cold email + LinkedIn message.
- **Day 3:** Follow-up email with one specific data point (e.g., the platform's own recent leak news, or a customer-reference offer).
- **Day 7:** Phone call attempt + voicemail referencing the original email.
- **Day 14:** Final follow-up — "closing this thread; happy to re-engage when you're ready."
- **Day 90:** Re-open if no response and no negative signal (per Bali Playbook §11).
