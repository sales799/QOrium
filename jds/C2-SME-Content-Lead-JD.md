## About QOrium

QOrium is the world's first enterprise-grade Question-Bank-as-a-Service for the hiring assessment industry. We supply fresh, validated, anti-leak-rotated questions to assessment platforms, enterprises, and staffing firms. Every question flows through a 7-stage Content Engine that combines AI speed with human rigor: AI authoring, self-critique, SME review, psychometric validation (IRT), live calibration, release, and continuous anti-leak monitoring. Our customers rely on us to answer "is this question actually measuring what we think it measures?" — and we prove it via Item Response Theory calibration and forensic leak detection.

## The Opportunity

By Month 3, QOrium must ship **5,000 validated questions** across Wave 1 (tech core: Backend, Frontend, Data, DevOps). By Month 6, we expand to Wave 2 (SAP, Oracle, Salesforce, Mobile, QA). By Month 12, we're at 40,000+ questions across 4 waves and 200+ roles.

The SME Content Lead owns the quality bar. You define what "validated" means, build the SME reviewer network (30 contractors by M3, 100 by M6), drive the question-editing workflow, and hand off to the I/O Psychologist for IRT calibration. You're not writing 5,000 questions yourself — you're designing the system that makes SMEs incredibly efficient at validating and improving AI-drafted questions.

This role is critical because content quality is our only moat against established platforms. If our questions feel generic or poorly designed, customers will leave. If they feel like the highest-quality technical questions on the market, customers will lock in and expand.

## What You'll Own

- **Content quality standard and rubrics** — Define the acceptance criteria for each format (MCQ: distractor plausibility, complexity of language; Coding: test case coverage, edge-case handling, reference solution clarity; SJT: scenario realism, response-option distinctiveness). Document rubrics in Notion/Markdown so every SME applies the same standard. Update rubrics quarterly based on feedback.

- **SME contractor network (hiring + management)** — Source 30 SME contractors by M3 across tech stacks (Java/Spring, Python/Django, React, Node.js, SQL, SAP ABAP, Salesforce, Oracle, DevOps, QA). On freelance platforms, university alumni networks, Talpro referrals. Vet their experience, onboard them on QOrium's process, manage ongoing feedback and compensation (~₹500–2,000 per validated question depending on complexity).

- **Question-editing workflow (end-to-end)** — Own the SME review loop in the admin console. SMEs see AI-drafted questions, critique scores (ambiguity, distractor quality, edge cases, bias, leak risk), and can accept/edit/reject. You oversee feedback cycles, identify trends (e.g., "all SMEs are flagging this format," "this role is harder than we expected"), and iterate.

- **Wave 1 Tech Core launch** — Ship 5,000 questions in Backend (Java/Python/Node), Frontend (React), Data (SQL/Python), DevOps by Month 3. You own the content schedule, SME capacity planning, and quality bar enforcement. No wave launches below your acceptance threshold.

- **Handoff to I/O Psychometrist** — Once SMEs validate, questions go to the I/O Psychologist for IRT calibration (Item Response Theory difficulty estimation). You document the handoff criteria, provide calibration feedback, and integrate psychometric findings back into your rubrics. This is a tight feedback loop.

- **Question diversity & bias auditing** — Ensure questions don't have gendered language, regional assumptions, or hidden prerequisites that advantage certain demographics. Use bias-detection rubrics and sample-audit every 500 questions for blind spots.

- **Role-graph alignment** — Work with the Senior Engineer to map every validated question into the role-graph taxonomy (role × skill × difficulty × format × language). Identify gaps (e.g., "we have 40 Senior Backend Engineer questions but only 5 for DevOps in that seniority"), and backfill.

- **Quarterly wave scoping** — Plan Waves 2, 3, 4 (new formats, new roles, new stacks). Recommend role priorities based on customer demand signals and market gaps. Present findings to the CDO quarterly.

## Must-Have Qualifications

- **7+ years in technical assessment authoring OR staffing-side test design.** You've written or edited technical questions for hiring assessments, training certifications, or corporate technical tests. You understand what makes a question discriminative (separates strong from weak candidates).

- **Deep knowledge across multiple tech stacks.** Backend (Java/Python/Node/Go), Frontend (React/Vue), Data (SQL/Python/Spark), DevOps (Kubernetes/Terraform/Cloud). You don't need to code in all of these, but you need enough depth to review a Kubernetes YAML question or a React state-management question and say "yes, this is a good question" or "no, this has X problem."

- **Question editing at scale.** You've edited hundreds of questions. You know how to give feedback that improves the question without rewriting it entirely. You've iterated with remote teams and managed quality across a large pool of contributors.

- **Rubric design & quality-bar discipline.** You've built grading rubrics, defined acceptance criteria, and enforced consistency across a reviewer pool. You've documented your standards clearly enough that new reviewers can apply them without ambiguity.

- **Comfort with Item Response Theory (conceptually).** You don't need a statistics degree, but you should understand that IRT gives us "difficulty b parameter" and "discrimination a parameter" — and why those matter for a good assessment. You should be able to read a calibration report and say "questions with discrimination < 0.3 are poor signal; let's re-examine them."

- **Bias detection.** You've thought about how questions can inadvertently favor certain demographics (gender, nationality, educational background, socioeconomic status). You can spot biased language and suggest fixes.

## How We Work

**7 Offices:** QOrium operates under the Talpro Universe Constitution v2.0. The CDO office (your reporting line) owns content quality, anti-leak enforcement, and psychometric validation. The Senior Engineer (CTO office) builds the platform; you ensure the content it delivers is world-class. These functions work tightly together.

**Weekly cadence:** Monday strategic 1:1 with CDO (30 min). Wednesday content pipeline review (throughput, quality flags, SME feedback). Friday wave planning + next-week prep. Async collaboration with Senior Engineer + I/O Psych on hand-offs.

**No-fiction culture:** Every claim we make about question quality — "SME-validated," "IRT-calibrated," "90th-percentile difficulty" — is auditable. You're the source of truth for content claims.

**Quality gates:** Every wave (Wave 1, 2, 3, 4) passes through a formal IdeaForge gate at Month 3, 6, 9, 12. You'll present quality metrics (pass rates, SME productivity, bias audit results, IRT findings). Gates are not blockers; they're alignment checks with the business.

**SME collaboration:** You'll work closely with the I/O Psychologist (contractor at first, FTE by Month 9) on the calibration loop. Monthly sync to review calibration findings and adjust rubrics. Quarterly deep-dive on bias audits and remediation.
