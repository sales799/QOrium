## About QOrium

QOrium is the world's first enterprise-grade Question-Bank-as-a-Service for the hiring assessment industry. We supply fresh, validated, anti-leak-rotated questions to assessment platforms, enterprises, and staffing firms. Our Stack-Vault SKU is for GCCs, large enterprises, and IT services — they buy exclusive, watermarked question libraries aligned to their specific tech stacks (SAP, Salesforce, Oracle, Java, Cloud, etc.). Every Stack-Vault customer gets a confidential library they own, refresh cycles (quarterly), and forensic leak detection: if one of their questions leaks externally, we prove which customer it came from.

The enterprise market is our most defensible segment. Deals range from ₹10L–₹1Cr+ annually. Sales cycles are 4–9 months. But the margin and durability are exceptional: once a customer's engineering team validates QOrium's content quality, they don't leave.

## What You'll Own

- **Prospect identification & outreach** — Build and maintain a list of 30–50 Tier-A GCC targets (Bosch, Siemens, JPMC, Goldman Sachs, Capgemini, Walmart, Target, Wells Fargo, Optum, etc.). Research TA leadership, technical hiring volume, current assessment spend. Outreach via LinkedIn, warm intros (CEO), NASSCOM events. Qualification: >1,000 hires/year, multi-stack hiring, existing technical assessment spend >₹50L/year.

- **Consultative discovery** — 3–5 discovery calls per prospect. Uncover: current assessment pain (candidate fraud, question repetition, generic content). What assessment spend they have today. Which tech stacks matter most. Their procurement timeline. Decision authority (TA Head, CHRO, CFO, CTO). You're painting the problem they're trying to solve, not pitching a solution yet.

- **Sample pack production & review cycle** — Work with the Senior Engineer + SME Content Lead to produce a 50-question sample pack on the prospect's highest-volume role (e.g., Senior Salesforce Developer at Bosch). Deliver it 1 week after discovery. Facilitate the prospect's engineering panel review (typical: 2-week review period). Answer technical questions from the panel. The goal: engineering panel says "yes, this would have caught the bad candidate."

- **Scoping & proposal development** — Once sample pack is endorsed, scope the full Stack-Vault engagement: How many roles? Which ones? Which difficulty mix? Quarterly refresh vs annual? Localization needs (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu)? Watermarking scope? Custom naming/branding? Build a detailed proposal with pricing (anchor: ₹40L/year for Enterprise-tier, ₹10L for Department-tier). Walk the prospect through ROI math: cost-per-assessment on a generic platform (Mettl) vs cost-per-assessment on Stack-Vault (fixed + all questions exclusive).

- **Procurement & contract negotiation** — Navigate the prospect's MSA template, security review, DPA (Data Processing Agreement) signing. Typical timeline: 4–8 weeks from proposal to PO. You own the back-and-forth with procurement, legal, and the CDO. Common asks: data residency, IP exclusivity language, SLA definitions, refresh cadence guarantees. Escalate to CEO for any pricing negotiation >10%.

- **Customer success handoff** — Once signed, own the transition into delivery. Work with the Senior Engineer to plan Wave 1 of their Stack-Vault (which roles to author first, timeline to 50 questions, timeline to 200, etc.). Attend kickoff call. Track delivery milestones. Be their first call if they hit friction. Goal: month 2–3 delivery success builds confidence for renewal.

- **Expansion & upsell** — By Month 9+ (after first Stack-Vault is live), pursue upsell: JD-Forge Reviewed subscription (for high-volume hiring drives), additional roles in Stack-Vault, expansion to other geographies. Track NRR (Net Revenue Retention). Target: 15%+ net expansion on existing logos.

## How We Work

**Reporting & accountability:** Weekly 1:1 with CEO (30 min). Weekly pipeline review on Mondays (deals by stage, at-risk flags, weekly activities, forecasted close dates in HubSpot). Monthly deal review + win/loss debrief. Quarterly business review and OKR reset.

**Sales enablement:** The CEO and CTO will support your first 2–3 deals (intro, discovery, sample pack review, contract negotiation). You'll own the discovery and scoping; CEO handles the first meeting (founder credibility); CTO handles technical Q&A. By your 3rd deal, you should run it independently with CEO in the background.

**Sales tools:** HubSpot CRM (reuse Talpro's existing instance). Templates for discovery calls, proposals, contract negotiation. Weekly activity tracking: emails sent, calls made, meetings held, proposals sent, deals closed.

**Compensation discipline:** Stack-Vault Enterprise tier (₹40L) never sells below ₹35L without CEO approval. This anchors the market and protects your commission. Expand margins via bundling (add JD-Forge subscription credits) rather than discounting.

**No-fiction rule:** Every claim you make to a prospect — "20+ programming languages," "24-hour leak rotation," "IRT-calibrated" — is backed by a live feature. The CTO will brief you on what's actually shipped before every customer meeting.
