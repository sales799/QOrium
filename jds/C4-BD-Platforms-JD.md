## About QOrium

QOrium is the world's first enterprise-grade Question-Bank-as-a-Service for the hiring assessment industry. We supply fresh, validated, anti-leak-rotated questions to assessment platforms, enterprises, and staffing firms. Our ReadyBank API SKU is for assessment platforms (HackerRank, Mettl, HackerEarth, iMocha, WeCP, Adaface, Xobin, etc.). Platforms integrate our REST API into their question library, get access to 40,000+ calibrated questions, and outsource content authoring and anti-leak rotation to us.

The platform motion is high-leverage: one integration unlocks 10,000+ end-customer assessments per year. It's also the longest sales cycle (6–12 months), but the longest LTV. If WeCP or iMocha integrates QOrium, they're locked in strategically — their engineers depend on our API.

## What You'll Own

- **Platform landscape mapping** — Identify 30–50 assessment platforms globally and in India. Tier them: Tier 1 (HackerRank, CodeSignal, Codility, SHL, Talogy — hard, VC-backed, own content roadmaps), Tier 2 (Mettl, iMocha, Mercer, Xobin, Adaface, Testlify), Tier 3 (Glider AI, WeCP, HirePro, mid-market platforms). Estimate their content team size, library depth, anti-leak readiness. Monthly competitive scan per §10.3 of QOrium Constitution.

- **VP Product/Chief Content Officer outreach** — Own relationship-building with platform decision-makers (VP Product, Head of Content, CEO). Use founder intros (CEO) for Tier 1/2. Cold LinkedIn + industry events for Tier 3. Typical decision-maker: 12+ years in product/engineering, concerned about library freshness, anti-leak, unit economics of in-house content teams.

- **Executive pitch & positioning** — You'll run 45-min to 60-min executive pitches to 10–15 platforms per year. Pitch centers on: (a) content unit economics (your cost-per-question is 10x lower than in-house), (b) anti-leak rotation (continuous refresh, 24-hour critical-leak SLA), (c) API simplicity (3 endpoints, <2s latency), (d) risk mitigation (we warrant content originality). Position QOrium as a complementary content layer, not a replacement for their platform.

- **Technical evaluation facilitation** — Platform engineering teams will need to evaluate: API latency, data format compatibility, integration effort (est. 4–8 weeks), rate limiting, SLA compliance, security posture (we pass their security review). You'll coordinate with the Senior Engineer to answer tech questions, provide test environments, and support integration planning.

- **Pilot scoping & contracting** — Scope a 6-month pilot: usage limits (e.g., 10,000 questions/month for Starter tier), defined success metrics (e.g., "platform users report improved question quality on NPS; content team effort on question curation drops 30%"), pricing ($50K–$150K depending on tier), term, and expansion path. Draft MSA, handle security review, facilitate signature.

- **Pilot support & success metrics** — Own the pilot delivery. You'll attend weekly pilot sync calls, track usage metrics, gather platform feedback, and identify friction. Your goal: prove that QOrium's content + anti-leak capabilities justify the ongoing investment. Typical KPIs: questions-per-month delivered via API, platform user feedback on question quality, platform content-team cost savings (estimated), leak-detection improvements.

- **Pilot-to-annual conversion** — At Month 5–6 of the pilot, run a formal evaluation: Did the platform hit their success metrics? Is their content team happy? Do they want to expand (more questions, new geographies, new formats)? Then propose the annual contract (3-year preferred, 1-year minimum) and negotiate expansion terms.

- **Year 2 expansion strategy** — Document playbook: how long a typical pilot takes, what success looks like, how to expand from Growth to Enterprise tier. Plan the next 5 platform targets. Identify patterns (e.g., "all Tier 2 platforms care about anti-leak; Tier 1 cares about unit economics").

## How We Work

**Reporting & accountability:** Weekly 1:1 with CEO (30 min). Monday pipeline review (deals by stage, outreach activity, at-risk flags, forecasted close dates). Monthly win/loss debrief. Quarterly business review and OKR reset.

**Sales enablement:** CEO will conduct founder-to-founder intros (especially for Tier 1/2 platforms). You own the discovery and scoping. CTO will join technical evaluation calls to answer architecture questions. By deal 2–3, you should drive them independently with CEO in the background for final negotiations.

**Sales tools:** HubSpot CRM. Sales playbook from the Bali Sales Playbook (§4). Templates for executive pitches, technical evaluation checklists, pilot contracts, success metric definitions. Weekly activity tracking.

**Partnership value & expansion:** After signing, you own the ongoing relationship. Quarterly business reviews, expansion conversations (new geographies, new formats, higher tier), and reference-building (platform wins are your best marketing for the next platform).

**Competitive discipline:** The platform motion is a 6–12 month cycle. Expect some losses (platform decides to build in-house, funding dries up, market shifts). You need a healthy pipeline: 5–7 active conversations to close 1 deal.
