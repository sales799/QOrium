## Why this role exists

QOrium has authored **1,300+ technical assessment questions** at v0.6 schema across 13 sub-skills/domains (Wave-1 Java/Python/React/SQL-Data/DevOps/Salesforce/AWS/AIPE + Wave-2 SAP-ABAP/OHCM/CPQ/Finacle/Embedded-Auto). All are AI-drafted, IRT-parameterised, and waiting for a human SME to flip them from `v0.6 AI-drafted` → `released`.

Without an SME Lead, every customer pitch hits the same wall: *"Is this content expert-validated?"* — and the honest answer is no. This role is the gatekeeper that converts our content from "AI says it's right" to "expert says it's right and stakes their reputation."

It's also the human partner to the I/O Psychologist contractor (Wave-3 native authoring per Constitution v2.1 ratified 2026-05-08; ₹65L Year-1 budget).

---

## What you'll do (first 90 days)

### Days 1-30 — Stabilise the pipeline
- Walk every shipped sub-skill (1,300+ questions) and use the admin-console SME Queue to flip ≥150 questions/week from `v0.6 AI-drafted` → `released`, blocked, or rewrite.
- Identify the lowest-quality 10% (worst clarity, weakest distractors, brittle rubrics) and either rewrite or quarantine.
- Co-design with the CTO Office a per-sub-skill "expert-grade exemplar" rubric — 3 reference questions per band that anchor what `released` should look like.
- Start building a peer-reviewer roster (paid hourly subject experts; 3-5 per sub-skill).

### Days 30-60 — Wave-3 partnership
- With the I/O Psych contractor (Wave-3 lead): co-design the cognitive-ability + personality-SJT + aptitude-SJT authoring template.
- Author the first 30-50 Wave-3 prototypes in pair with the I/O Psych; feed Reference Panel calibration runs.
- Establish the SME-vs-psychometrician boundary cleanly so neither role becomes the bottleneck.

### Days 60-90 — Operationalise
- Promote ~600 questions to `released` (50%+ of the library); document the rewrite-rate and quality-gate pass-rate trends.
- Hire and onboard 2-3 part-time peer reviewers (₹2-5K/question rates).
- Stand up a quarterly "content quality board" — 1 SME Lead + 1 I/O Psych + 1 CTO Office rep + 1 customer-success voice.

## Who you are

### Must-haves

- **8+ years** as a senior software engineer / engineering manager / staff engineer in any of: backend, frontend, data, DevOps, cloud, ML — *or* deep recruiting-tech experience having written / curated assessment items at scale (Mettl / SHL / iMocha / HackerRank-internal types qualify).
- **Strong written communication.** You can explain why a question is good, why it's bad, and how to fix it — in writing, in 3 sentences.
- **Cross-domain comfort.** You don't need to be expert in all 13 sub-skills, but you can spot a bad Java question even if you're a Python person.
- **Calibration mindset.** You believe a question's quality is measurable (clarity, discrimination, fairness, IRT fit) and you're allergic to "I think it's fine" without evidence.
- **Operational discipline.** You ship 30-50 reviewed items per day at quality, not 10 perfect ones plus 40 untouched.

### Nice-to-have

- Prior experience as I/O Psychologist, psychometrician, or formal exam-author (CFA, CPA, FRM, banking certs).
- Open-source contributor profile (or technical blog) we can read.
- Experience hiring + managing peer reviewers / contract subject experts.
- Experience with Bloom's taxonomy / SOLO taxonomy / Webb's DOK.

### Disqualifiers

- "I'll get the AI to do it." This is your job, not the AI's. AI helps; you decide.
- "Quality over speed always." Untrue for our stage. Speed AND quality, measured weekly.
- Inability to write a 1-paragraph rejection rationale that the question-author would learn from.

## How we're going to interview you

### Round 1: Async take-home (90 min, paid ₹3000)

We send 10 AI-drafted questions across 3 sub-skills. You return:
- Per-question: `released` / `revise` / `reject`, with a 3-sentence rationale per rejection or revise.
- A 200-word write-up: **"What is the strongest signal a hiring assessment question is high-quality, in your view?"**

Goal: speed × quality × judgment.

### Round 2: Live work session (60 min, video)

CEO + CTO Office + you. We pull up the admin SME Queue live, you walk us through how you'd review 5 questions in real time. We probe your thinking.

### Round 3: Reference + offer (30 min, video)

CEO + 1 reference call (we choose; you nominate 5, we pick 2 to call). Discussion of comp, scope, ramp.

Total: ~3 hours of your time. Decision in 7 days.

If "AI agent as a peer" sounds weird to you, that's fine — we'll send you the constitution before round 1. Most candidates find the framing clarifying once they read it.
