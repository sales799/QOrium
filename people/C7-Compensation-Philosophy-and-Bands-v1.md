## 1. Compensation Philosophy

QOrium's compensation model embodies three core principles that tie directly to our venture thesis and exit definition per Constitution Article IX (Project Completion = ≥$300M acquisition OR ≥₹3,000Cr IPO OR $50M ARR anchor).

### Principle 1: Below-Market Cash, Above-Market Equity

Year 1 hires are joining a venture in pre-revenue stage ($0 → target $420K ARR by M12, but starting from zero). We cannot match the cash bands of FAANG or even late-stage Indian startups. What we can offer is **meaningful equity in a defensible venture with a clear path to $50M+ ARR by Year 5**, which anchors a $300M–$1B acquisition or $3,000Cr IPO outcome.

**Rationale:** Founders and early engineers at AWS, Stripe, Notion, HubSpot, Freshworks all accepted 40–50% below-market cash in exchange for 0.1–0.5% equity (50–500 bps for engineering hire #1). That equity became worth $10M–$100M at exit. QOrium's venture is in a similar position: large TAM ($30B+ assessment market, 9–11% CAGR), differentiated moat (I/O psych + AI authoring + anti-leak rotation), clear GTM (3 parallel motions, traction from Day 1 via Talpro Customer Zero), and founder with distribution + Talpro network.

### Principle 2: Performance-Variable for AE/BD; Fixed for Engineering/Content

Sales roles (AE, BD) are compensated with a **strong variable component** tied to quota attainment and pipeline generation. This aligns seller incentives with revenue targets and keeps the company's unit economics predictable. Engineering and content roles are compensated with fixed cash + equity; variability is managed through annual refreshes and promotion gates, not through commission.

**Rationale:** Sales roles in high-velocity discovery mode (Recruiter motion, Enterprise introductions, Platform pilots) benefit from quota-driven urgency. Engineering and content work is collaborative and long-term (12+ months to ship a validated question library); individual bonus variability creates internal friction. Fixed compensation + equity alignment is cleaner.

### Principle 3: Transparency and Predictable Progression

All compensation bands and equity grants are shared with candidates in **Round 2 of the interview** (after IQ / culture fit screening but before final decision). We do not use equity or compensation as a negotiation lever at the final stage. We operate within published bands or offer the candidate the next-tier role at a higher band, but we do not "negotiate down the equity to make room for cash" or vice versa.

**Rationale:** Talpro Universe standard. Transparency builds trust. Candidates who accept our band are self-selecting for mission fit, not maximum negotiated cash.

## 2. Cash Compensation Bands (by Role)

All bands expressed in INR for India hires (the entirety of Year 1 hiring), USD noted for future US hires. Ranges anchor to **Indian senior tech market median ± 10%** per Naukri Tech Salary Report, AmbitionBox, and Levels.fyi India data (verified December 2025 – April 2026).

**Important:** These are INDICATIVE bands for operating plan. BEFORE extending any offer (Month 1 onward), source fresh market data from the three sources above to validate. Offer letters will cite the data sources used for each role.

### Engineering Roles

**Senior Engineer — Content Engine (IC4 equivalent)**

- Role: Full-stack TypeScript, AI pipeline, content engine architecture, owns anti-leak engine, mentors IC3
- Experience: 8+ years total; 3+ in AI/LLM pipelines or assessments; experience shipping to scale
- Cash band: ₹35L–₹50L/year (₹2.9M–₹4.2M; ~$43K–$60K USD)
- Rationale: Senior tech market in Bengaluru for IC4 engineers centers ~₹42L/year (Naukri 2026 data); we offer full band to attract one strong founder-track engineer

**Frontend Engineer (IC3 equivalent)**

- Role: React, Next.js, TypeScript; admin console + customer widget + SME interface
- Experience: 5–7 years; 2+ in modern React/Next.js; shipped B2B SaaS
- Cash band: ₹25L–₹35L/year (₹2.1M–₹2.9M; ~$30K–$42K USD)
- Rationale: Mid-senior market for IC3; align to Talpro Universe standard

### Content / Validation Roles

**SME Content Lead (IC3-equivalent content engineer)**

- Role: Defines question quality standard, owns contractor SME network, mentors SME reviewers, signals on content pipeline quality gates
- Experience: 8+ years as senior engineer OR 6+ years engineering + 2+ in assessment/validation; structured interview experience ideal
- Cash band: ₹28L–₹40L/year (₹2.3M–₹3.3M; ~$33K–$48K USD)
- Rationale: Content engineering is as complex as backend engineering; hire an engineer who moves into content, not a generalist

**I/O Psychologist (Contractor → FTE pathway)**

- Role: Contractor (M4–M9): design validation rubrics, oversee IRT calibration methodology, run Reference Panel sampling. FTE (M9+): hire content team, set comp tier strategy, regulatory defensibility
- Experience: M.A. or Ph.D. in I/O Psychology, Industrial/Organizational Psychology, or equivalent (e.g., psychometrics + 10 years in assessment); published work in item difficulty calibration ideal
- Cash band (FTE only): ₹40L–₹60L/year (₹3.3M–₹5M; ~$48K–$60K USD)
- Rationale: Rare skill in India; below-market for top-tier psych PhDs at global tech, but above market for India-based assessment roles. Contractor rates: ₹2.5L–₹4L/month (M4–M9 pilot).

### Sales Roles

**AE — Enterprise (on-target earnings)**

- Role: Closes GCC + large enterprise Stack-Vault engagements; owns 5–10 enterprise logos by M12; works with CEO on large deals in M1–M3
- Experience: 5+ years selling into HR/TA leaders; 3+ in India GCC / IT services context; $500K+ ACV track record
- Cash base: ₹30L–₹40L/year (₹2.5M–₹3.3M; ~$36K–$48K USD)
- Variable (on-target, 60% of OTE): ₹18L–₹24L/year (₹1.5M–₹2M; ~$21.6K–$28.8K USD)
- OTE (base + target): ₹48L–₹64L/year (₹4M–₹5.3M; ~$57.6K–$76.8K USD)
- Rationale: Sales roles need cash velocity to fund personal overhead (travel, entertainment, home office). Base is below Tier-1 SaaS (which pays ₹50L+ base for this profile), but OTE with quota attainment is competitive. Quota: 3–5 logos / ₹1.5Cr ACV by M12.

**BD — Platforms (on-target earnings)**

- Role: Builds partnerships with assessment platforms (HackerRank, Mettl, iMocha tier); negotiates pilot terms; closes API contracts
- Experience: 5+ years partnerships or BD in HR Tech; 2+ in India market; $50K–$200K ACV track record
- Cash base: ₹28L–₹38L/year (₹2.3M–₹3.2M; ~$33K–$46K USD)
- Variable (on-target, 50% of OTE): ₹14L–₹19L/year (₹1.2M–₹1.6M; ~$16.8K–$22.8K USD)
- OTE (base + target): ₹42L–₹57L/year (₹3.5M–₹4.8M; ~$49.8K–$68.4K USD)
- Rationale: Platform deals are smaller ACV than enterprise, longer sales cycle. Variable is lower % OTE (50% vs AE's 60%) to reflect the slower ramp. Quota: 3 pilots + 1–2 annual contracts by M12.

## 3. Equity Bands (as % of authorized pool)

QOrium's authorized share capital is **₹1 Cr** (10 million shares @ ₹10 FV). Recommended ESOP pool: **12% authorized (₹12L shares), 8% reserved for first 12 hires** (as per Constitution Article VII governance norms for venture-stage companies).

**Vesting: 4-year cliff + linear vest, 1-year cliff.** All equity grants include:
- 1-year cliff (no vesting in Y1; if the hire leaves before M12, they forfeit all equity)
- 3-year linear vest post-cliff (equal monthly vesting M13–M48)
- Standard acceleration: 50% of unvested equity accelerates upon company acquisition

| Role | Min | Max | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Senior Engineer #1** | 50 | 100 | Founding IC4 engineer; owns anti-leak moat; typically 0.1–0.5% for engineering hire #1 at seed-stage ventures |
| **SME Content Lead** | 30 | 60 | IC3 engineering equivalent; content expertise is core IP; second engineering hire typically 50% of first |
| **AE Enterprise** | 30 | 60 | Sales hire #1; high risk (depends on network + execution); bands similar to IC3 for symmetry |
| **BD Platforms** | 25 | 50 | Longer ramp than AE; slightly lower band to reflect slower revenue contribution M1–M6 |
| **Frontend Engineer** | 25 | 50 | IC3 engineer; second engineering hire after Senior Engineer |
| **I/O Psychologist (FTE conversion)** | 20 | 40 | Specialized hire; comes in as contractor (0 equity), converts to FTE at M9 with smaller grant (rare specialist market) |

**Total pool allocation M0–M12:** ~300 bps (2.5% of authorized) reserved for hires 1–6 + option pool for future. Leaves 4.5% for hires 7–12 and 5% unallocated for future rounds / advisor / employee option pool.

## 4. Variable Compensation for AE + BD

### Target Compensation Structure

**AE Enterprise:**
- Base: ₹35L (midpoint) = 60% of OTE
- On-target variable: ₹23.33L = 40% of OTE
- **Total OTE: ₹58.33L/year**

**BD Platforms:**
- Base: ₹33L (midpoint) = 67% of OTE
- On-target variable: ₹16.5L = 33% of OTE
- **Total OTE: ₹49.5L/year**

### Quota + Commission Structure

**AE Enterprise Quota:**
- Annual quota: **₹1.5Cr ACV** (3 Stack-Vault Department-tier + overflow spillage)
- Commission: **12% of ACV signed in cash**
- Example: Close ₹1.5Cr in stack vaults → ₹18L variable (~12% of ₹150L base salary) → total comp ₹53L

**BD Platforms Quota:**
- Annual quota: **$200K ACV** (3 pilot contracts @ avg $65K/year, or 1–2 annual contracts @ $100K+)
- Commission: **10% of ACV signed in cash** (lower % to reflect longer cycle)
- Example: Close $150K in API contracts → $15K variable (~9% of base) → total comp slightly below target

### Pay-on-Collection vs Pay-on-Bookings Stance

**Policy: Pay-on-Bookings (PO issued or contract signed).** Commission is earned when the customer signs, not when cash hits the bank. This aligns seller incentives with deal closure, not collections (which are often customer-delayed due to AP cycles).

**Exception for Stack-Vault (whitelabel) engagements:** First half of commission (6%) paid on signing; second half (6%) paid on 50% of engagement completion (after first batch of questions delivered, around M3–M4 of the engagement). This prevents gaming multi-quarter deals.

### Clawback Rules

Commission clawed back if:
1. Customer terminates within 6 months of signing (full clawback)
2. Customer renews with 50% discount or churn threat (50% clawback on that renewal cycle)
3. Deal fraudulently signed (e.g., no actual procurement authority at customer) — full clawback + termination for cause

Otherwise, all commission vests with the salesperson for the full contract term they closed.

## 5. Refresh + Promotion Philosophy

### Annual Review (M12, M24, M36)

At the end of each year, all compensation is reviewed. Raises (cash or equity) are allocated based on:
- **Performance** (achieved quota, shipped milestones, quality signals)
- **Market correction** (if salary bands have drifted since hire date, adjust upward)
- **Promotion readiness** (from IC3 → IC4, from AE → Principal AE, etc.)

### Equity Refreshes

Upon promotion or significant role expansion:
- **At promotion:** Grant **25% of original grant** as a refresh (on a new 4-year vest schedule)
- **Example:** Senior Engineer hired M2 at 80 bps (vest starts M2, cliff M13). Promoted to IC5/Principal role at M24. Receives 20 bps refresh grant starting M24 (new cliff at M35, vest until M48 alongside original grant).

### Cliff + Acceleration

- **Standard acceleration:** 50% of unvested equity accelerates upon **company acquisition** at any valuation
- **Double-trigger acceleration:** No acceleration on founder exit / management change within same company (protects employee retention post-acquisition)
- **Change of control clause:** If acquisition is deemed "hostile" (forced by board, not negotiated), 100% acceleration (rare; requires legal review)

## 7. Compensation Governance

### Authority Matrix

| Decision | Authority | Process |
|---|---|---|
| **Offer for hires 1–6** | CEO + CTO joint | Both must approve; no unilateral hiring |
| **AE/BD quota changes** | CEO solo | Adjusts based on pipeline; communicated in monthly review |
| **Comp exceptions (e.g., accelerated vesting)** | CEO + Board (CTO + Advisor if applicable) | Rare; only for retention in extreme scenarios |
| **Annual refreshes (M12, M24)** | CEO + CTO | Formulaic per policy; no negotiation |
| **Promotion (IC3 → IC4)** | CEO + SME lead (for engineering) | Merit-based on demonstrated impact |

### Deferred Governance

**Compensation committee** — Deferred to Year 2 after company scales to 10+ employees. Until then, CEO + CTO manage all comp decisions directly. This keeps governance light and prevents bureaucratic creep early.

## 8. Drafting Notes (5 items to validate before Month 1 offers)

1. **Bands validation:** Before extending any offer, source fresh market data (Naukri, AmbitionBox, Levels.fyi) from April–May 2026 to confirm the ± 10% anchors. If market has drifted, communicate the delta to candidates.

2. **Equity SAR vs ESOP decision:** Currently assumes ESOP (stock option pool). If the cap table moves to SAR (Stock Appreciation Rights) — more common in India post-2025 regulatory clarity — the vesting and acceleration mechanics change. Confirm with legal counsel by M0-end.

3. **Tax structuring:** India tax on ESOPs is complex (capital gains on exercise, income tax on vest). Work with Talpro's tax advisor to determine optimal grant timing and clawback / forfeiture language to minimize employee tax burden while protecting company.

4. **Perquisites philosophy:** Should QOrium offer gym membership, health insurance top-up, work-from-home stipend, tech allowance (laptop refresh)? Talpro Universe standard is minimal (health insurance only). Recommend matching Talpro standard to keep cost transparent.

5. **Remote-stipend policy:** Blueprint contemplates India-based hires (Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune). If candidates from Tier-2 cities require remote work, do we offer ₹5K–10K/month connectivity + home office stipend? Defer to M1 hiring manager discretion; not pre-committed.
