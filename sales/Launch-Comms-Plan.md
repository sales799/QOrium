## Executive Summary

QOrium launches in three phases over 12 weeks:

1. **Stealth Phase (Weeks 1–3):** Customer Zero validation, internal alignment, soft launch to Talpro network.
2. **Soft Launch (Weeks 4–6):** Controlled rollout to Bosch & Biz4Tech, case studies, thought leadership.
3. **Public Launch (Weeks 7–12):** Public website, press, community activation, product hunt launch.

Success metrics: 10+ customers by Month 3 end, 50+ revenue-generating transactions, 1,000+ community members, 5+ pieces of published thought leadership.

## Phase 1: Stealth (Weeks 1–3)

**Objective:** Validate product-market fit with Customer Zero (Talpro India), de-risk launch, build internal consensus.

### 1.1 Week 1: Internal Kickoff

**Audience:** Talpro Universe leadership, QOrium core team
**Format:** In-person + async Slack

**Deliverables:**

- **Internal Memo (Day 1):** CEO announces QOrium to Talpro Universe team via all-hands + memo. Tone: "We're solving a $2B+ problem in technical recruiting. Here's our edge."
- **Team Kickoff (Day 2):** 90-minute in-person session with exec team + CMO + VP Sales + VP Product. Align on 3-month roadmap, message pillars, GTM risks.
- **Sales Enablement Deck (Day 3):** 15-slide internal deck covering 3 SKUs, unit economics, target personas, objection handling.
- **Slack #qorium-launch channel (Day 1):** Async updates, resource links, wins celebration.

**Key Messages for Internal Team:**
- QOrium is a B2B2B SaaS venture: we license question banks to platforms, staffing platforms, and enterprises.
- 3 SKUs: ReadyBank (shared commodity), JD-Forge (on-demand custom), Stack-Vault (exclusive IP).
- Moat: anti-leak engine (daily crawl + semantic similarity) rotates compromised questions 3–7 days.
- Year 1 revenue target: ₹3.5Cr ARR ($420K).

### 1.2 Week 2: Customer Zero (Talpro India) Validation

**Audience:** Talpro India CEO, Operations Head, Hiring Team
**Format:** In-person + Slack

**Deliverables:**

- **Executive Briefing (Day 1):** CMO + VP Sales + CTO present to Talpro India leadership. Walk through product, roadmap, Customer Zero expectations.
- **Product Demo (Day 2):** Full walkthrough of ReadyBank + JD-Forge with live data. Show anti-leak dashboard. Gather feedback on feature gaps.
- **Onboarding Playbook (Day 3–5):** Hand-hold Talpro India through API setup, first 50 question imports, first JD-Forge order. Document all friction points.
- **Internal Case Study Draft:** Capture Talpro India's hiring volume, question library size, pain points. Frame as "how we reduce leak risk by 90% vs. public question banks."

**Success Criteria (Week 2):**
- Talpro India team can execute full workflow independently.
- Identified 5+ feature requests or UX fixes; prioritize for Month 1.
- Customer Zero NPS ≥ 7 on initial experience.
- Product feedback validated hypothesis: anti-leak engine + JD alignment > existing solutions.

### 1.3 Week 3: Soft Outreach to Target Customers

**Audience:** Bosch, Biz4Tech, Recruiter Network
**Format:** Email + personal calls

**Deliverables:**

- **Outreach Email (Day 1):** Personalized email to Bosch VP Talent + Biz4Tech CEO + 3–5 key recruiters. Subject: "New Question-Bank Solution Built for Technical Hiring." Tone: exclusive early-access offer, invite to 30-min demo.
- **Demo Scheduling (Day 2–5):** Schedule 3–5 initial discovery calls. Goals: validate pain point (question leakage, time-to-market), understand current workflow, identify objections.
- **Bosch Deep-Dive (Day 3):** If positive signal, schedule 60-minute working session with Bosch hiring manager + tech lead. Walk through their hiring workflow, show JD-Forge capability.
- **NDA Preparation (Day 5):** For any customer showing strong intent, prepare lightweight NDA for Stealth phase feedback.

**Key Message (Outreach):**
"We've built QOrium — a question bank platform with an anti-leak engine that auto-rotates compromised questions. 10+ of your questions are on Reddit/GitHub right now; we catch & replace them in <7 days. Early adopters get 50% discount on Year 1 ARR + exclusive feature input."

**Success Criteria (Week 3):**
- 3–5 customer discovery calls scheduled.
- 1 customer (Bosch or Biz4Tech) signals serious intent to pilot.
- Talpro India Customer Zero is live on ReadyBank, beginning to generate data.

## Crisis Communications Playbook

### Scenario 1: Product Security Incident (Question Leak Not Caught)

**Detection:** Customer reports question on Reddit that should've been caught by anti-leak engine.

**Response (within 4 hours):**
1. Triage: confirm leak is real, determine customer impact, assess severity (1–5 scale).
2. CEO + CTO + CMO emergency standup (30 min).
3. Internal Slack #incident: post status update, impact statement, remediation plan.
4. Direct customer outreach: CMO calls customer sponsor, explains root cause, offers remediation (question rotation + credit).
5. Post-mortem: schedule 48-hour post-incident review.

**Public Messaging (if leaked):**
"We detected a question leak faster than our SLA. This is exactly why we built QOrium's anti-leak engine. We've automatically rotated the question and offered our customer a credit. Learn more: [blog post]."

**Tone:** Transparent, problem-solving, not defensive.

### Scenario 2: False Leak Alert (Question Flagged as Leaked But Isn't)

**Detection:** Customer reports false positive on anti-leak alert.

**Response (within 2 hours):**
1. Investigate: confirm it's a false positive, determine cause (e.g., semantic model too aggressive).
2. Mark as false positive in system (train model with feedback).
3. Customer outreach: apologize, explain cause, offer action (improve model).
4. Public messaging (if disclosed): "We're improving our model to reduce false positives while maintaining leak detection accuracy."

**Tone:** Apologetic, learning-oriented.

### Scenario 3: Major Customer Churn (e.g., Bosch Exits)

**Detection:** Customer requests early exit / non-renewal signals.

**Response (same day):**
1. CMO + VP Sales immediately engage: discovery call to understand pain points (is it product, price, integrations, support?).
2. Escalation: if escalation is needed, CMO briefs CEO; consider executive intervention.
3. Remediation: offer service credit, expedite roadmap feature, assign dedicated success manager.
4. Internal comms: don't publicize; track in dashboard.

**Messaging (if public):** Only if customer volunteers to share reason. Otherwise, no comment.

### Scenario 4: Negative Press / Industry Criticism

**Detection:** Article questioning QOrium's anti-leak effectiveness, or questioning market demand.

**Response (within 24 hours):**
1. Assess: is the criticism valid? Is it based on outdated info or misunderstanding?
2. Data response: prepare blog post or LinkedIn article with data refutation (cite examples, metrics, case studies).
3. CEO engagement: CEO considers direct response (thoughtful, non-defensive) or amplification of data response.
4. Internal: brief team, don't amplify criticism internally.

**Tone:** Data-driven, not emotional.
