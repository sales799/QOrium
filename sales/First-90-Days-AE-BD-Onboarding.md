## 1. Overview & Philosophy

The first 90 days are critical for both AE Enterprise and BD Platforms leads. The goal is not to close a deal in 90 days (that's Month 4–6 milestone), but to:

1. **Understand the product deeply** (SKUs, pricing, anti-leak story, competitor positioning)
2. **Build the initial prospect list** (30+ targets, tier-ranked, research complete)
3. **Execute outreach at scale** (10–15 prospects contacted, 2–3 in active discovery)
4. **Internalize the sales playbook** (pipeline stages, MEDDPICC, pricing discipline, forecasting)
5. **Establish CEO alignment** (weekly standups, escalation protocols, culture fit confirmed)

This document provides a **day-by-day, week-by-week, phase-by-phase** sequence. Each milestone has clear success criteria.

## 4. Week 2–4: Outreach Phase

### Week 2: First Outreach Wave

**Goal: 10–15 prospects contacted; 3–5 discovery calls scheduled**

**Day 1–2 (Mon–Tue): Email campaign**
- Draft 3–5 personalized cold emails (CEO reviews 2 before you send; then you freestyle)
- Email template: name-drop (CEO or Talpro connection), problem statement (content staleness / leak risk), unique value (exclusive + anti-leak), 1-line CTA (15-min discovery call)
- Send: 5 cold emails Mon, 5 cold emails Tue
- Sequence active in HubSpot: auto-follow-up emails on Day 5, 10, 15

**Day 3–4 (Wed–Thu): LinkedIn outreach**
- LinkedIn connection requests to 5–10 TA heads / VP Products with personalized message (copy of cold email or variant)
- Goal: 30–50% acceptance rate by end of week

**Day 5 (Fri): CEO-assisted intros**
- CEO sends 3–5 warm intro emails on your behalf (you cc'd)
- Template: "Hi [TA Head], I'd like to introduce [You], who's joining QOrium as our Enterprise Account Executive. We're building Stack-Vault specifically for GCCs like you. 15-min discovery call this coming week?"
- Calendar: schedule 30-min slots next week for these warm intro discovery calls

### Week 2 Check-In (Fri 10am, 30 min)

**Call with CEO:**
- Outreach volume: how many contacts made? response rate?
- Discovery calls confirmed: how many, which prospects?
- Learnings: any signals emerging? (e.g., "all Tier-A prospects care about leak detection")

### Weeks 3–4: Active Outreach + First Discovery Calls

**Ongoing outreach (email + LinkedIn):**
- Maintain 5–10 email touches per week (new outreach + follow-ups)
- Maintain 3–5 LinkedIn touches per week

**First discovery calls (Week 3–4):**
- Schedule: 2–3 discovery calls per week
- Attendees: You + CEO (or Senior Engineer if technical Q&A heavy)
- Duration: 30–45 minutes
- Agenda: Understand current assessment platform, pain (fraud, staleness, cost), stack, hiring volume, procurement timeline, decision authority
- Outcome: If qualified, agree on next step (sample pack request, product demo, whitepaper)
- Follow-up: You send recaps within 24 hours; flag stage transition in HubSpot

**Pilot debrief (every Mon 10am):**
- CEO + AE/BD review calls from prior week
- What went well? What to adjust?
- Any prospects to re-prioritize or de-prioritize?

### End of Week 4 (Day 30 Milestone)

**Criteria for success (Day 30):**
- ✅ 30–50 prospect list built, tier-ranked, research complete
- ✅ 10–15 prospects contacted (email + warm intro)
- ✅ 3–5 discovery calls held (or scheduled pending confirmation)
- ✅ 1–2 prospects in Sample Pack Review phase (for AE) OR Technical Evaluation phase (for BD)
- ✅ HubSpot pipeline seeded with 10+ deals
- ✅ Weekly standups + CEO alignment established
- ✅ Confidence: product knowledge, sales playbook, pricing discipline understood

**Day 30 reflection call (Friday, 4pm):**
- CEO + AE/BD + CFO (brief, 20 min)
- Report: "By today, we've contacted X prospects, held X discovery calls, and X are in active conversation. Forecast by Month 4: Y pipeline opportunities (₹50L+)."

## 5. Day 30–60: Sales Cycle Acceleration

### Goal: 1+ deals in Sample Pack Review (AE) or Pilot Scoping (BD); 3+ in Discovery

**Weeks 5–8 activity focus:**

**1. Deepen prospect relationships (from discovery → sample pack request / technical eval):**
- 3–5 follow-up calls per prospect (discovery, technical questions, stakeholder intro, sample pack review kickoff)
- Send value-adds between calls (competitor comparison, case study, technical brief)
- Track decision authority (map TA Head → CHRO → CFO chain)

**2. Sample pack coordination (AE focus):**
- Week 5: First qualified prospect (e.g., Bosch) requests sample pack
- Week 5–6: You coordinate with Senior Engineer + SME Lead to produce 50-question sample on prospect's top role
- Week 6–7: Prospect engineering panel reviews; you attend feedback call, answer technical questions
- Week 7–8: Document feedback; scope full Stack-Vault proposal based on panel feedback

**3. Technical evaluation (BD focus):**
- Week 5: First qualified platform (e.g., WeCP) confirms interest in API integration
- Week 5–6: Senior Engineer delivers test API credentials, Postman collection, OpenAPI spec
- Week 6–7: Platform engineering team evaluates; you join technical sync calls
- Week 7–8: Technical team provides integration estimate; you move to Pilot Scoping

**4. Proposal development (both):**
- Week 7–8: Draft first proposal (sample pack endorsed OR technical eval complete)
- You write proposal; CEO reviews and signs
- Send proposal + cover email within 5 business days of scoping call

**5. Continue outreach (maintain pipeline momentum):**
- Weeks 5–8: Introduce 5–10 new prospects (don't slow down during first deal cycle)
- Goal: By Day 60, you have 2–3 deals in Sample Pack Review / Pilot Scoping, 2–3 in Discovery, 5+ in Outreach

### Weekly standup (Mon 10am, 30 min):
- AE/BD + CEO
- Agenda: deal progression, blockers, next week priorities
- Forecast update: when do you expect first deal to close?

### Day 60 Check-In (Friday, 4pm):

**Call with CEO:**
- Status: X deals in active sample pack review / pilot scoping; Y in discovery
- Forecast: How confident are you in first close by Day 90? Or Day 120?
- Learnings: Patterns emerging? (e.g., "all prospects ask about SLA"; "Tier-A GCCs care more about watermarking than cost")
- Morale check: Getting support you need? Any blockers?

## 6. Day 60–90: Deal Closure + Pipeline Establishment

### Goal: 1st deal closure (or very close); 3+ deals in active conversation; established repeatable sales motion

**Weeks 9–12 activity focus:**

**1. Close first deal (or near-close):**
- Prospect in Scoping: finalize scope, pricing, contract terms
- Prospect in Negotiation: security review, legal review, signature (should be <4 weeks from proposal send)
- Expected close: Week 11–12 (Day 75–90)

**2. Negotiate pricing with discipline:**
- Use Bali Playbook discount guardrails
- Any discount >5%: notify CEO for approval (24-hour SLA)
- Any discount >10%: CEO + CFO approval required

**3. Keep first sample pack/pilot moving post-close:**
- Week 11–12: First deal closes (signature + payment)
- Week 12: Coordinate Wave 1 delivery kickoff (Senior Engineer + you + customer)
- Document success: "Bosch Stack-Vault signed ₹40L, Wave 1 delivery by Month 6"

**4. Maintain pipeline velocity:**
- Weeks 9–12: 2+ new deals entering Scoping phase (from your Week 5–8 sample pack reviews)
- Weeks 9–12: Keep 3–5 new prospects in Discovery (cold outreach Week 5–6 now entering discovery calls)
- Weeks 9–12: Outreach continues (5–10 new contacts per week)

**5. Introduce independent deal motion:**
- Week 10: You run first discovery call WITHOUT CEO present (CEO observes or skips)
- Week 11: You run first scoping/proposal call WITHOUT CEO
- Week 12: You close first deal with CEO in background (available for escalation if needed)
- Goal: By Day 90, you're 80% independent; CEO is 20% advisory/escalation

### Weekly standup (Mon 10am, 30 min):
- AE/BD + CEO
- Deal status, forecast, activity, wins

### Bi-weekly deep dive (Week 10, 12):
- AE/BD + CEO + CTO (45 min)
- First deal debrief: what worked, what to improve?
- Next 5 prospects: prioritization, strategy, pricing positioning

### Day 90 Milestone Review (Friday, 3pm)

**Call with CEO + CFO + CTO (60 min):**

**Presentation structure:**

1. **Deal scorecard:**
   - First deal signature & payment date: ___
   - First deal value: ___ (target ₹40L for AE; $150K for BD)
   - First deal gross margin: __% (target >80%)

2. **Pipeline snapshot:**
   - Total prospects in conversation: ___ (target 4–5)
   - Forecast (weighted): ___ (target ₹50L+ for AE; $300K+ for BD)

3. **Activity summary:**
   - Contacts made: ___ (target 25+)

   - What's working? (e.g., "warm intros have 80% discovery conversion; cold emails 20%")
   - What's not? (e.g., "Tier-A GCCs need 4 stakeholder calls before sample pack request")
   - Competitive dynamics emerging?

5. **Next 90-day plan (Day 91–180):**
   - How many more deals by Day 180? (target: 1 close + 2–3 in scoping by Day 180)
   - How will you accelerate? More outreach, better targeting, CEO co-selling strategy?
   - Quota path: on pace for Year 1 quota? (AE: ₹400K; BD: $240K)

### Day 90 Go/No-Go Evaluation

**Success criteria:**
- ✅ 1st deal closed (signature + payment on account) **OR** very close (in final negotiation, <2 weeks to close)
- ✅ 4+ total prospects in conversation (2+ in Scoping/Negotiation, 2+ in Discovery)
- ✅ Demonstrated independent deal motion (you run majority of calls without CEO present by Week 12)
- ✅ Forecast: ₹50L+ pipeline (AE); $300K+ pipeline (BD) by end of Q2
- ✅ Sales playbook internalized: you can explain MEDDPICC, pipeline stages, pricing, forecast methodology unprompted

**If YES on all 5:** Proceed to Day 91–180 (ramp ends; quota phase begins Month 7)

**If NO on 2+:** CEO + CTO debrief: what's blocking progress? Is role fit good? Any changes needed to support success?

## 8. Failure Modes & Intervention Triggers

### Red Flags (Trigger CEO Intervention)

| Flag | Symptom | Response |
|---|---|---|
| **No deal closure by Day 120** | 1st deal should close by Day 90; if slipping past 120, something is wrong | CEO calls prospect to understand hold-up; consider changing your strategy or rep |
| **<3 active prospects by Day 60** | Pipeline should have 3–5 prospects by Day 60 | CEO coaches on outreach volume + qualification; may need to adjust target list |
| **Consistent rejection of MEDDPICC qualification** | You're advancing unqualified prospects (e.g., <₹50L spend) to scoping unnecessarily | CEO reviews your last 5 prospects; resets qualifying rules; coaches on discovery depth |
| **Discount requests >10%** | You're struggling with pricing; trying to win via discount instead of value | CEO + CFO review your sales motion; may need to reposition value or deprioritize prospect |
| **Declining activity** | Outreach volume drops Week 5–6 (distracted by first deal cycle) | CEO notices in Monday standup; refocuses on activity targets (5–10 outreach per week minimum) |
| **Lack of CEO co-sell participation** | You're not scheduling CEO check-ins or sample pack reviews | CEO checks in: are you hoarding deals or struggling to escalate? Reset collaboration model |

### Intervention Playbook

**If <2 success criteria met by Day 60:**
1. Stop new outreach (Week 7–8) — focus 100% on closing first 2–3 deals in pipeline
2. CEO co-sells every discovery call Week 7–8 (maximize close rate)
3. Weekly strategic reviews (vs. monthly) to unblock deals

**If <1 deal closed by Day 120:**
1. Honest conversation: Is this the right role/rep? Or is the market slower than expected?
2. Options: (a) extend ramp, (b) bring in second rep to share pipeline, (c) change target segment, (d) part ways

**If activity drops but pipeline strong:**
1. This is normal (focused on first deal close) — support it
2. BUT: re-activate outreach by Week 12 to refill pipeline for Month 4–6

## 9. Drafting Notes

### Note 1: CEO Co-Sell Cadence
The goal by Week 12 is independence, but CEO should stay engaged for: (a) first 2–3 deals (credibility), (b) any deal >₹50L for AE / >$200K for BD (pricing escalation), (c) stuck deals (customer relationship reset). By Month 4–6, CEO presence should drop to 20% of your calls.

### Note 2: Sample Pack / Technical Eval Timing
Day 30 success criteria says 1–2 in Sample Pack/Tech Eval phase. This is aggressive. If you hit Day 30 with 0, don't panic — discovery calls take 2–4 weeks, then another 1–2 weeks to request sample pack. Expect first sample pack request mid-Week 5, not Week 4. Adjust timelines accordingly during Day 30 check-in.

### Note 3: Remote vs. In-Office Onboarding
This sequence assumes some flexibility (could be remote or in-office). If in-office, add: (a) weekly in-person office lunches with team, (b) office hours with CTO Office staff for ad-hoc questions, (c) Friday team socials. If remote, schedule: (a) fortnightly video coffee with CEO, (b) monthly Zoom team meetings, (c) async communication discipline (all key decisions + decisions recorded in Slack or email for async review).
