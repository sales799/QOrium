## POST TEXT (paste into LinkedIn)

We just open-sourced QOrium's 92-point release quality gate.

Yes, before we shipped a single line of customer-facing code, we wrote a 92-point checklist. People asked why.

Here's what's in it:

→ 10 pillars (Build · Security · Monitoring · Compliance · Performance · AI Stack · Enterprise Security · Enterprise Ops · Enterprise Reliability · Governance)
→ +12 QOrium-specific items (IRT calibration coverage ≥80%, AI plagiarism detection ≥93% public benchmark, 24-hour anti-leak rotation operational, ATS connector smoke-test pass, IO-Psych contractor sign-off, no-fiction rule audit on every external claim…)
→ 6 auto-fail criteria — a single one means no release

Threshold: ≥88/92 to ship a customer-facing release. Lower than that, two-person sign-off mandatory; below 60% on any pillar = remediation queued.

Why publish it?

QOrium is a product line of Talpro India Pvt Ltd. We sell to hiring leaders at Bosch, Wipro, TCS — companies whose hiring decisions cost crores when wrong. They don't trust unverifiable claims. They trust audit trails.

The scorecard is the audit trail.

Every customer-facing release saves a completed scorecard at /governance/quality-gate-runs/YYYY-MM-DD.md. When a customer asks "why should we trust your library?" we don't argue. We hand them the last 12 scorecards.

This is what "quality is the product" looks like when the product is a question library.

Open-source link in comments. Free to copy if you're building HR-tech, B2B SaaS, or any compliance-heavy enterprise product.

Customer Zero with Talpro India launches this month. First Bosch GCC discovery call queued.

— Bhaskar
Founder, Talpro India Pvt Ltd / QOrium product line
