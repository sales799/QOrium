## BODY (~600 words)

### The problem QOrium solves

Public technical assessment libraries have a structural reliability problem. A Senior Java question added to a major assessment platform's library on Monday is typically available verbatim on Reddit, Stack Overflow, GitHub, and Indian recruiting Telegram groups by Friday. Internal CTO Office research conducted on a sample of 10 popular Java mid-senior questions found a median time-to-locate of 47 seconds via standard search engines; all 10 questions were found within four minutes.

The economic cost of this is substantial. A single false-positive senior engineering hire — caused by a candidate who scored well on a leaked assessment but cannot perform in the role — costs Indian IT services firms an estimated ₹50 lakh in opportunity cost per incident, including ramp-up time, project rework, and team morale impact.

### What QOrium does differently

QOrium operates three product lines (SKUs):

1. **ReadyBank** — a shared, IRT-calibrated question library spanning eight technical sub-skills today (Senior Java, React/JavaScript, SQL/Data, DevOps/SRE, Senior Salesforce, Senior Python, Senior AWS, AI Prompt Engineering) with 160 candidate-ready questions in v0.6. Annual subscription ₹4,999/month for individual recruiters; $5,000-$25,000/year for assessment platform integrations.

2. **JD-Forge** — on-demand assessment generation custom-fit to a customer's job description, available in three tiers: $49 (AI-only, 24-hour SLA), $199 (AI + SME review, 48-hour), $499 (AI + SME + IRT calibration + custom export, 5-day).

3. **Stack-Vault** — exclusive customer-owned question libraries authored against a customer's specific tech stack. Anti-leak rotation cycle of 24 hours; per-candidate watermarking for forensic leak detection. Annual contract: ₹10 lakh (Department tier) to ₹1 crore+ (Group tier).

All three SKUs share QOrium's anti-leak engine: continuous web crawl monitoring, deterministic per-customer variant generation, and a 24-hour rotation cycle that ensures any leaked question is replaced before the next candidate session.

### Customer Zero

Talpro India operates QOrium as a product line, not a separate legal entity. Talpro India is also QOrium's first customer. From Month 1, Talpro's internal hiring pipeline for top five technical roles — Senior Java, React, SQL/Data, DevOps, and Senior Salesforce — runs on QOrium-delivered assessments. The Customer Zero relationship provides QOrium with structured weekly feedback on signal quality, false-positive rates, and candidate experience.

### Founder context

QOrium was conceived by Bhaskar Anand, founder of Talpro India Private Limited, after fifteen years of running an IT staffing firm and observing assessment-content leakage compound year over year. "Every recruiting platform's library leaks faster than its content team can refresh it," Anand said. "The platforms aren't the problem — they solve the platform problem. The content layer was missing. We built QOrium to fix the content."

### Engineering quality bar

QOrium ships under a 92-point release quality gate published openly at qorium.online/insights/92-pt-gate. The gate covers ten pillars (Build, Security, Monitoring, Compliance, Performance, AI Stack, plus four enterprise-grade categories) and twelve QOrium-specific items including IRT calibration coverage of at least 80% on released items, AI plagiarism detection benchmarked at 93% or higher against public benchmarks, and a recursive No-Fiction Rule requiring every external claim to be backed by a tool call or live source.
