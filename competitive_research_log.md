## Purpose

This log is the **canonical scratchpad** for all competitive intelligence at QOrium. Per Constitution Article III (Truth Hierarchy) and Article V SO-25, this is the source of truth for the live state of the competitive landscape — superseding any audit-doc-derived list. The competitor relationship classification in Constitution §2.7 reflects what is **codified** as of the most recent ratification; this log reflects what is **observed** between ratifications.

**Discipline:**

1. Append-only: numbered findings, never edited after entry.
2. Every finding cites a primary source (URL, press release, filing, customer reference). No second-hand claims without verification.
3. Material moves trigger MANTHAN re-validation per SO-25.
4. The relationship classification table in Constitution §2.7 is updated only through Constitutional Amendment cycles (Article XI) — never ad-hoc edits to this log.

**Watch focus** (per Constitution §10.3):

- Acquisitions, market exits, strategic pivots
- Pricing changes >20%
- Library size step-changes >50%
- Founder/CEO transitions
- Funding rounds (Series and amount)
- Product release notes for AI-augmented content authoring at production scale
- Customer reference shifts (e.g., partnership announcements with content suppliers)

### #001 · 2025-01-16 · Byteboard → Karat acquisition

**Class change:** Byteboard moved from "partnership candidate" → **OBSOLETE** (acquired).

**Source:** Karat press release, January 16, 2025; subsequent product integration announcements through Q1 2025.

**Material:** Yes. Byteboard had been a content-quality benchmark in QOrium's positioning (per audit-doc-derived comparisons). Acquisition foreclosed the partnership and moved the conversation up the stack to Karat.

**Action taken:**

- Relationship classification updated in Constitution v2.0 (May 2026 ratification cycle) — Karat reclassified from TIER 1 to TIER 2 API CUSTOMER (multi-product post-acquisition).
- Treated Karat as multi-product partner; outreach now goes to Karat enterprise BD, not Byteboard team.

**MANTHAN re-validation:** Not triggered retroactively (acquisition predates SO-25 codification). Going forward, identical class change would trigger MANTHAN immediately per SO-25.

```

**Material:** Yes / No · Reason: <why>

**Action taken:**
- <relationship classification update via Constitutional Amendment or no change>
- <outreach implications>
- <pricing implications>
- <positioning implications>

**MANTHAN re-validation:** Triggered / Not triggered · Reason: <why>
```

**Material move thresholds** (Constitution §10.3 §4):

- Acquisition or merger involving any competitor in §2.7 table
- Market exit (shutdown, public statement of withdrawal)
- Strategic pivot (positioning shift, ICP shift, pricing-tier introduction or removal)
- Pricing change >20% on any published tier
- Library size step-change >50% (announced or measurable via API access)
- Founder or CEO transition
- Funding round (Series and amount, even if private)
